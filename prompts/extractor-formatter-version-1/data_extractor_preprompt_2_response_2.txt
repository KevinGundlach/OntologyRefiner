### Missing Critical Variables for Your Schema

* **Reference Electrode:** This is arguably the most important missing piece. Pitting potentials are meaningless without knowing the reference scale (e.g., SCE, SHE, Ag/AgCl). Your model must normalize all extracted potentials to a single scale.
* **Surface Finish / Roughness:** Corrosion initiation is highly dependent on surface topography. You need to extract whether the sample was polished (and to what grit/micron level), ground, or left "as-received". 
* **Sample Geometry / Form:** As the Carroll & Lynskey paper strongly highlights, the geometry (e.g., a 0.25 mm wire loop vs. a flat sheet or rod) dramatically affects the likelihood of surface flaws and, consequently, the measured pitting potential.
* **Deaeration / Aeration:** Was the test solution purged with nitrogen or argon? Dissolved oxygen levels drastically alter the cathodic kinetics and passivity breakdown.
* **Pre-test Conditioning:** Did the researchers hold the sample at a cathodic potential to clean the surface? Did they pre-passivate it in nitric acid? How long was it left at the open-circuit potential before scanning?
* **Definition of Breakdown ($E_p$):** How did the authors define the pitting potential? Was it a visual observation, a sudden spike in current, or a specific current density threshold (e.g., reaching **10 µA/cm²**)?

**[Begin Prompt Template]**

**Role:** You are an expert Materials Scientist and Data Extraction Agent. Your task is to extract highly structured data from the provided materials science research paper regarding metal alloy corrosion testing. 

**Goal:** Extract a hierarchical dataset mapping "Base Materials" to "Conditioned Materials" to "Experiments." Your primary objective is to capture *all* variables that could influence the corrosion pitting potential ($E_p$), including hidden or easily overlooked variables. Ensure you scan all text, tables, plots, and figures. If exact numbers for pitting potentials are only available in plots, provide your best numerical estimate based on the axes.

**Output Format:** Provide the extracted information strictly as a valid JSON object matching the hierarchical structure defined below. Do not include markdown formatting outside the JSON block.

**Extraction Schema Instructions:**

**1. Base Materials (List)**
Extract every distinct metal alloy studied as a base material. For each, provide:
* `base_material_id`: The identifier used in the paper (e.g., "AISI 316L", "W5", "Duplex 2205").
* `composition`: The precise chemical composition, including trace elements. Include the unit of measurement (e.g., "wt %", "at %").
* `alloy_type`: A brief string describing the metallurgical category (e.g., "Austenitic stainless steel", "Ferritic", "Ni-base alloy").
* `microstructure`: A brief description of the microstructure if mentioned (e.g., "pearlite martensitic", "elongated grains").
* `other_base_info`: Any other relevant base material details (e.g., inclusion density, grain size, manufacturing origin).

**2. Conditioned Materials (List - Nested under each Base Material)**
Extract the specific variations of the base material resulting from treatments or manufacturing. For each, provide:
* `conditioned_material_id`: The specific identifier if provided (e.g., "W502", "W549").
* `process_description`: How the material was manufactured or shaped (e.g., "hot rolled to 0.100 inch sheet", "drawn into 0.25 mm wire"). Include sample geometry.
* `surface_finish`: Describe the surface preparation (e.g., "polished to 600 grit SiC", "wheel ground", "as-received").
* `heat_treatment`: Details of thermal processing (e.g., "annealed 16 hours at 1200°C in argon, water quenched").
* `other_conditioned_info`: Any other treatments, such as chemical pickling or passivation prior to testing.

**3. Experiments (List - Nested under each Conditioned Material)**
Extract every distinct corrosion test performed on the conditioned material. For each, provide:
* `test_procedure_type`: The electrochemical method used (e.g., "Potentiodynamic polarization", "Scratch method").
* `scan_rate`: The polarization scan rate (e.g., "60 mV/min").
* `pre_test_conditioning`: Details on cathodic cleaning or open-circuit hold times prior to the scan.
* `breakdown_definition`: How the pitting potential was defined (e.g., "current density reached 10 µA/cm²").
* `test_solution`: The electrolyte composition (e.g., "1.0 M NaCl", "Synthetic seawater").
* `chloride_concentration`: Explicitly extract the chloride/halide ion concentration and unit.
* `pH`: The pH of the solution.
* `temperature`: The temperature of the solution.
* `aeration_status`: Was the solution deaerated (e.g., "purged with N2 for 24h") or aerated? Mention dissolved oxygen levels if given.
* `reference_electrode`: The reference electrode scale used (e.g., "SCE", "SHE"). *CRITICAL FIELD*.
* `pitting_potential_value`: The numerical value of the pitting potential measured. (Estimate from plots if necessary).
* `pitting_potential_unit`: The unit of the pitting potential (e.g., "mV", "V").
* `other_experiment_info`: Any other experimental setup details (e.g., crevice-free electrode assembly used, flow velocity).

**[End Prompt Template]**
