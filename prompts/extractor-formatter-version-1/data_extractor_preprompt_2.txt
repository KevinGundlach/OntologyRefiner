I've attached a few example research papers for reference. 

We are particularly interested in discovering potential hidden variables - details that may affect pitting potential but which are often overlooked by experts. Thus, it is important to extract *all* information provided that could possibly affect the resulting pitting potentials measured, even if that information isn't explicitly requested in the prompt. 

In each paper there is a list of "base materials" - the metal alloys that are studied. For each "base material" please extract the following information:

* base material identifier, if provided.

* alloy type: Please provide a brief paragraph describing the type of alloy. For example, whether it's an austenitic steel, whether it's a duplex, a stainless, a ferritic, martensitic, etc...

* microstructure: Provide a brief paragraph describing the alloy's microstructure. For example, whether it has a pearlite martensitic microstructure.

* other information: In addition to the above, extract any other information provided in the paper for each base material that is possibly relevant for the analysis of corrosion pitting potential.

For each "base material" we have one or more "conditioned materials". That is, the result of any kind of manufacturing process, heat treatments, chemical alterations or other modifications applied to the base material that could affect its corrosion pitting potential.

* other information: In addition to the above, extract any other information provided in the paper for each conditioned material that is possibly relevant for the analysis of corrosion pitting potential.

For each "experiment" please extract the following information:
