## 1. High-Nickel Austenitic Series Alloys

**Shared Alloy Characteristics for this Series:**

* 
**Alloy Type**: Austenitic stainless steel.


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: Induction-melted in an argon atmosphere and cast in air using the split heat technique. Ingots were hot-forged and hot-rolled to 0.76 cm thick, then cold-rolled to 0.38 cm.


* 
**Microstructure**: Completely austenitic.


* 
**Heat Treatment**: Annealed at 1150°C for 1 hour and water-quenched.


* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: Electrodes were mounted in acrylic plastic. Surfaces were polished to a 0.3-micron alumina finish. For pitting scans, electrodes were masked with electroplater's tape (Scotch 470) to expose a 0.3 cm diameter circle. Unmasked 0.5 x 0.6 cm electrodes were used for primary passivation tests. Exposure test coupons were 2.5 x 3.8 x 0.3 cm with a 0.64 cm suspension hole.
