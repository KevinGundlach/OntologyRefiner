# Base Material

- chemical_composition: Fe 17.87, Ni 18.79, Cr 16.65, Cu 10.17, Al 8.63, Si 8.99, Co 18.86, C 0.03, P 0.006, S 0.003 (wt.%) [Page 3, Table 1]
- material_classification: High entropy alloy (HEA) / Multi-component glassy alloy [Page 1, Abstract]
- microstructural_features: The dendritic phase is composed mainly of a non-crystalline (amorphous) phase with a small amount of body-centered cubic (BCC) structure (lattice constant 2.91 Å). The interdendritic phase has an amorphous structure containing small amounts of nano-scale crystalline face-centered cubic (FCC) precipitates. [Page 1, Abstract; Page 7, Section 3.1]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: As-cast HEA (Cu0.5NiAlCoCrFeSi) [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- manufacturing_process_description: Prepared from a mixture of pure elements and smelted by arc melting on a water-cooled copper hearth under a purified argon gas atmosphere. Repetitively melted and solidified by turning the ingots over to obtain a completely alloyed state. Solidified in a water-cooled copper mold. Bulk ingots were cut using a water-cooled grinding wheel into pieces 2 mm thick, 1.91 cm long by 0.89 cm wide, and cold mounted using an epoxy resin. [Pages 3-4, Section 2.1]
- heat_treatment_parameters: Not specified in text (tested in the as-solidified/as-cast state). [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Mechanically polished with 600–1500 grit emery cloths, thoroughly cleaned with distilled water and acetone, and dried in air. Prior to testing, the specimen was cathodically polarized at -2.0 V with respect to the corrosion potential ($E_{corr}$) for 1 minute to reduce any oxides on the exposed surface. [Page 6, Section 2.4.1]
