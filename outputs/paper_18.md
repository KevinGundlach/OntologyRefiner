- chemical_composition: Atomic mole ratios: B 0: Al0.5CoCrCuFeNi (0 atom % B); B 0.2: Al0.5CoCrCuFeNiB0.2 (3.5 atom % B); B 0.6: Al0.5CoCrCuFeNiB0.6 (9.8 atom % B); B 1.0: Al0.5CoCrCuFeNiB1.0 (15.4 atom % B) [Page 2, Table I]
- material_classification: High entropy alloy [Page 2, Abstract]
- microstructural_features: Multicomponent solid solution with mixed fcc and bcc phases. The addition of boron produces Cr, Fe, and Co borides, making the fraction of Cr outside borides precipitates scant. [Page 2, Abstract & Introduction]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: As-cast Al0.5CoCrCuFeNiBx [Page 2, Experimental]
- manufacturing_process_description: Melted in an induction furnace at 1600°C after purging with argon. Fe and Ni were melted first, followed by the addition of Cr, Co, FeB, Cu, and Al according to their melting points. The melt was then poured into a shell mold preheated at 1100°C to form 8 × 7 × 14 cm ingots. [Page 2, Experimental]
- heat_treatment_parameters: Not specified in text. [Page 2, Experimental]
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Cylindrical specimens were cut from the bulk material by electric arc line into pieces 3 mm thick and 8 mm in diameter. Specimens were cold-mounted using an epoxy resin, resulting in an exposed surface area of 0.5 cm². Further polishing steps are not specified in text. [Page 2, Experimental]
