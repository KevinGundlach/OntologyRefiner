# Group 2: Binary Cr-Fe Alloys

**1. Alloy Name/Identifier:** Binary Cr-Fe Alloys
**2. Alloy Type:** Binary Iron-Chromium Alloys (Ferritic/Martensitic)
**3. Alloy Composition:** 12% to 57.8% Cr (weight %), balance Fe. (Iron was deoxidized and decarburized to approximately 0.001-0.002% C).
**4. Alloy Description & Processing:** Prepared by vacuum melting pure components in dense alumina crucibles. Melt drawn into 7 mm diameter Vycor tubes and water quenched. Ingots >40% Cr were machined directly from the homogenized ingot due to difficulty swaging. Lower % Cr-Fe ingots were cold rolled to 0.25 cm or swaged to ~0.45 cm diameter rods.
**5. Microstructure:** Not specified.
**6. Heat Treatment:** Homogenized in Ar or He at 1050°–1100°C for several hours. Swaged/rolled alloys were subsequently annealed at 1000°–1050°C and water quenched.
**7. Surface Preparation & Geometry:** Electrodes measured ~2 cm long and were either 0.4 cm diameter rods or 0.5 cm wide x 0.2 cm thick. Mounted by threaded attachment to a nickel wire, pressed against a glass tube separated by a Teflon gasket. Abraded to a final 3/0 emery paper and pickled in 15% HNO3 + 5% HF at 80°C for 5 minutes.
**8. Test Procedure & Environment:**
*   **Methodology:** Potentiostatic polarization. Polarized at least noble potential in passive region for $\ge$15 min. Potential advanced in 50 mV stages (5 min per stage) to find non-steady state onset, followed by holding potential for 10 hours or longer to establish steady-state critical pitting potential ($V_c$).
*   **Electrolyte:** Aqueous NaCl
*   **Chloride/Halide Concentration:** 0.1 N NaCl
*   **pH:** Not specified.
*   **Temperature:** 25° \pm 0.2°C
*   **Aeration:** Deaerated with purified nitrogen (passed over Cu at 450°C) prior to test; no nitrogen flow during actual measurements.
*   **Scan Rate:** 50 mV increments every 5 minutes (for non-steady state $V'_c$).
**9. Pitting Potential ($E_{pit}$):** *(Steady-state Vc, implied V vs NHE/SHE, estimated from Figure 1)*
*   ~12% Cr: ~0.35 V
*   ~20% Cr: ~0.35 V
*   ~25% Cr: ~0.45 V
*   ~30% Cr: ~0.75 V
*   ~40% Cr: ~0.95 V
*   48.8% Cr: Pitting observed at ~1.0 V
*   57.8% Cr: No pitting observed (corrodes uniformly via transpassivity/chromate formation).
**10. Other Relevant Metrics:** Not specified.

# Group 6: Quaternary 15% Cr, 13% Ni, Mo Stainless Steels

**1. Alloy Name/Identifier:** 15% Cr, 13% Ni, Mo Stainless Steels
**2. Alloy Type:** Austenitic Stainless Steels (Mo-alloyed)
**3. Alloy Composition:** 15% Cr, 13% Ni, varying Mo (0%, 0.43%, 1.0%, 1.3%, 1.43%, 2.0%, 2.4% weight %), balance Fe.
**4. Alloy Description & Processing:** Vacuum melted, drawn into Vycor tubes, quenched. Swaged to ~0.45 cm diameter rods.
**5. Microstructure:** Single-phase structure.
**6. Heat Treatment:** Annealed at 1050°C and water quenched. (Note: Kept low carbon 0.001-0.002% to avoid intergranular attack, avoiding slow cooling).
**7. Surface Preparation & Geometry:** ~0.45 cm diameter rod, ~2 cm long. Teflon gasket setup. Abraded to 3/0 emery paper, pickled in 15% HNO3 + 5% HF at 80°C for 5 minutes.
**8. Test Procedure & Environment:**
*   **Methodology:** Steady-state potentiostatic polarization AND Exposure Testing (Immersion).
*   **Electrolytes:** Tested in four different environments: 0.1N NaCl, 0.1N NaBr, 10% FeCl3, and 10% FeBr3.
*   **Aeration:** Deaerated with purified N2 prior to polarization tests.
*   **Scan Rate:** Steady-state holding used.

**9. Pitting Potential ($E_{pit}$) & Immersion Results:**

*   **Experiment A: 0.1 N NaCl at 25°C** (Estimated from Figure 6)
    *   0% Mo: ~0.25 V
    *   ~1.0% Mo: ~0.3 V
    *   1.3% Mo: ~0.32 V
    *   2.0% Mo: ~0.35 V
    *   2.4% Mo: No definite critical potential (transpassive uniform corrosion, no breakdown).

*   **Experiment B: 0.1 N NaCl at 0°C** (Estimated from Figure 6)
    *   0% Mo: ~0.78 V (Marked noble shift at 0°C vs 25°C)
    *   ~1.0% Mo: ~0.4 V
    *   1.3% Mo: ~0.32 V (Values at 0°C and 25°C merge here)

*   **Experiment C: 0.1 N NaBr at 25°C** (Estimated from Figure 8)
    *   0% Mo: 0.54 V (Explicitly stated in text)
    *   1.43% Mo: ~0.48 V
    *   2.4% Mo: No breakdown (Indefinite results, corrodes without pitting).

*   **Experiment D: 0.1 N NaBr at 0°C** (Estimated from Figure 8)
    *   0% Mo: ~0.3 V
    *   Higher Mo %: Indefinite results, transpassive state reached at low potentials, corrodes uniformly.

*   **Experiment E: Immersion in 10% FeCl3**
    *   *0°C:* 0% Mo (No pitting observed); 2.4% Mo (Definite pitting after two days).
    *   *25°C:* 0% Mo (Pitted); 2.4% Mo (Did not pit within two days).

*   **Experiment F: Immersion in 10% FeBr3**
    *   *0°C and 25°C:* 0%, 1.4%, and 2.4% Mo alloys all pitted within two days.

**10. Other Relevant Metrics:** 1.43% Mo alloy in 0.1N NaCl at 25°C showed anodic current oscillations beginning at ~0.55 V up to 1.5 V, attributed to simultaneous molybdate ($MoO_4^{--}$) and chromate ($CrO_4^{--}$) formation.
