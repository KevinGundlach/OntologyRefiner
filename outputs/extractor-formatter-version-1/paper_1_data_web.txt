### Binary Cr-Fe Alloys

#### Experiment: Potentiostatic Polarization in NaCl

* 1. Alloy Name/Identifier: Binary Cr-Fe alloys.


* 2. Alloy Type: Binary iron-chromium alloys.


* 3. Alloy Composition: 12% to 57.8% Cr by weight, Fe balance. Carbon content ranges from 0.001-0.002%.


* 4. Alloy Description & Processing: Vacuum melted from pure electrolytic iron (deoxidized and decarburized) and high purity chromium in dense alumina crucibles. The melt was drawn into 7 mm diameter Vycor tubes and water quenched. Ingots were homogenized in argon or helium at 1050-1100°C for several hours. Alloys containing less than 40% Cr were cold rolled to 0.25 cm or swaged to roughly 0.45 cm rods. Alloys with more than 40% Cr were difficult to roll and were machined directly from the ingot.


* **5. Microstructure**: Not specified.
* 6. Heat Treatment: Annealed at 1000-1050°C and water quenched. A subsequent anneal was not necessary due to the low carbon content.


* 7. Surface Preparation & Geometry: Electrodes measured approximately 2 cm long and were either 0.4 cm in diameter or 0.5 cm wide by 0.2 cm thick. They were abraded to 3/0 emery paper and pickled in 15% HNO3 with 5% HF at 80°C for 5 minutes. The electrodes were mounted with a threaded member and pressed against a glass tube separated by a Teflon gasket.


* **8. Test Procedure & Environment**:
* 
**Methodology**: Potentiostatic anodic polarization.


* 
**Electrolyte**: 0.1N NaCl.


* 
**Chloride/Halide Concentration**: 0.1N Cl-.


* **pH**: Not specified.
* 
**Temperature**: 25°C.


* 
**Aeration**: Deaerated with purified nitrogen prior to testing, but with no gas flow during actual measurements.


* 
**Scan Rate**: Steady-state values ($V_{c}$) were obtained by holding the potential at a fixed value for 10 hours or longer.




* 9. Pitting Potential ($E_{pit}$): All values vs. H2 Scale. ~0.2V for 12% Cr (estimated from Figure 1); ~0.24V for 23% Cr (estimated from Figure 1); ~0.58V for 29% Cr (estimated from Figure 1); ~0.89V for 39% Cr (estimated from Figure 1); ~0.91V for 48.8% Cr (estimated from Figure 1). Values >0.9V (transpassive, no pitting) for 57.8% Cr and pure Cr.


* **10. Other Relevant Metrics**: Not specified.

* 
**Aeration**: Deaerated with purified nitrogen prior to testing.


* 
**Scan Rate**: Steady-state values obtained by long-term holds.




* 9. Pitting Potential ($E_{pit}$): All values vs. H2 Scale.


* 
**At 25°C**: ~0.28V for 0% Mo (estimated from Figure 6); ~0.3V for 0.43% Mo (estimated from Figure 6); ~0.35V for 1% Mo (estimated from Figure 6); ~0.4V for 1.43% Mo (estimated from Figure 6); ~0.53V for 2% Mo (estimated from Figure 6); ~0.75V for 2.4% Mo (estimated from Figure 6).

* 10. Other Relevant Metrics: Current oscillations were observed for the 1.43% Mo alloy starting at 0.55V up to 1.5V, and for the 0% Mo alloy starting at 0.7V, attributed to molybdate and chromate formation.
