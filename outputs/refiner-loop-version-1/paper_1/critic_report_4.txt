### 3. Formatting & Structure Violations
*   **Nested Reagents in Pickling:**
    *   **JSON:** `"reagent_name": "HNO3 + HF pickle", "concentration": {"value": 15.0, "unit": "% HNO3"}`.
    *   **Violation:** This is a flat string for a mixture. It should be a list of reagent objects. 
    *   **Recommended Structure:** 
        ```json
        "chemical_treatments": [{
            "reagents": [
                {"name": "HNO3", "concentration_value": 15, "concentration_unit": "%"},
                {"name": "HF", "concentration_value": 5, "concentration_unit": "%"}
            ],
            "temperature_value": 80, "temperature_unit": "°C"
        }]
        ```
*   **Metadata in Alloy Names:**
    *   **JSON:** `"alloy_name": "15% Cr, 13% Ni Stainless Steel (0C)"`.
    *   **Violation:** Condition metadata (temperature) is baked into the identity string. The identity should be the alloy, and conditions should remain in the `experimental_conditions` block.
*   **Flat Text for Steady State Criteria:**
    *   **JSON:** `"steady_state_criterion": "10-hr or longer period of constant polarization"`.
    *   **Recommended Structure:** This should be structured: `"steady_state_duration_value": 10, "steady_state_duration_unit": "hr"`.

### 4. Missing Information
*   **Gas Purge Protocol:** The paper specifies that nitrogen was passed over Cu at 450°C **"but without flow of nitrogen during the time of actual measurements"** (p. 792). The "stagnant" vs "flowing" status of the gas during the test is a critical electrochemical parameter.
*   **Electrode Geometry:** The paper provides specific dimensions: "0.45 cm diam rods" and "0.4 cm diam or 0.5 cm wide by 0.2 cm thick" (p. 791). This is important for calculating current density.
*   **Homogenization Step:** The Extractor captured cold rolling and annealing but missed the initial homogenization: "homogenized in A or He, usually at 1050°-1100°C for several hours" (p. 791).
*   **Pitting Onset Current Density:** Page 792/793 mentions pitting or transpassivity starts being indicated by a "steadily increasing current" at specific thresholds (e.g., 0.1 mA/cm˛ or 0.4 mA/cm˛). These are valuable result metrics.
*   **Surface Exposure Method:** The use of a "Teflon gasket" and a "glass tube" to ensure only specific surfaces are exposed (p. 792) is a key metadata point for experimental validity.

### 5. Schema Recommendations for the Refiner Agent
1.  **Merge Signs into Values:** Convert all `pitting_potential` objects to a single signed float field.
2.  **Deconstruct Mixed Reagents:** Create a `reagents` array within `chemical_treatments` to handle multiple acids (HNO3, HF) separately with their own concentrations.
3.  **Standardize Composition Units:** Default all `%` units to `wt%` where text refers to alloy composition.
4.  **Extract Durations from Strings:** Ensure the "10-hr" steady-state period and the "5 min" step interval are captured as numeric duration fields with units.
5.  **Expand Sample Capture:** The paper contains complete data for the Cr-Ni binary series (Fig. 2) and the Ni-effect on 15Cr-Fe (Fig. 5). These should be extracted as individual samples.
6.  **Add 'Passivity Status':** Add a field for samples like 57.8% Cr-Fe that do **not** pit but undergo transpassive dissolution (CrO4-- formation), as the absence of pitting is as important as its presence for GNN boundaries.
