This evaluation report assesses the Extractor Agent's performance in capturing data from the provided paper on critical pitting potentials.

### 1. JSON Validity: Pass
The provided JSON is perfectly formatted and follows valid syntax.

### 2. Hallucinations & Inaccuracies
*   **Alloy Identifier "Aluminum (Pure)":** The Extractor assigns a value of `100 wt% Al`. While listed as "Al" in Table I, the paper describes most alloys as prepared from "pure components," but doesn't explicitly state the Al in Table I (sourced from reference 3) is 100% pure.
*   **18-8 Stainless Steel Composition:** The Extractor provides `18` and `8` values for Cr and Ni. While implied by the name, the text describes it as a "commercial composition" (Table I footnote) and later mentions "Type 301" (page 793). The Extractor should mark these as nominal values or extract the specific "15% Cr, 13% Ni" composition used for the majority of the study's custom alloys.
*   **Mo-Ni Alloy Results:** The Extractor lists the methodology as "Potentiodynamic Anodic Polarization." However, the paper (page 792) explicitly states a **potentiostatic** method was used ("potential was then advanced in stages of 50 mv, allowing 5 min between each change").

### 3. Formatting & Structure Violations
*   **Combined Value/Unit Fields:**
    *   `"test_temperature": "25 C"` and `"0 C"`. This must be split into `"value": 25, "unit": "C"`.
    *   `"chlorine_concentration": "0.1 N"`. Should be `"value": 0.1, "unit": "N"`.
    *   `"temperature": "1000 C"` (Heat treatment). Should be `"value": 1000, "unit": "C"`.
*   **Flat Strings Needing Nested Structuring:**
    *   **Methodology:** `"Stages of 50 mV, 5 min intervals; steady-state observed over 10-hr period"`. This contains three critical parameters: `potential_step_value: 50`, `potential_step_unit: "mV"`, `step_duration_value: 5`, `step_duration_unit: "min"`, and `minimum_total_test_duration_value: 10`, `minimum_total_test_duration_unit: "hours"`.
    *   **Pre-passivation Steps:** `"pickled in 15% HNO3, 5% HF at 80 C for 5 min"`. This should be structured into a list of reagents with concentrations, temperature, and duration.
    *   **Dissolved Oxygen:** `"deaerated with purified N2"`. This should be structured: `{"purging_gas": "N2", "purity": "purified", "method": "passed over Cu at 450 C"}` (as per page 792).

### 4. Missing Information
*   **Carbon Content:** The paper explicitly mentions a very low carbon content for the prepared alloys: **0.001-0.002% C** (page 791, footnote 2) and **0.0015% C** for the 15-13 SS (page 794). This is critical for corrosion resistance and was missed in several alloy entries.
*   **Atmosphere during Homogenization:** The paper specifies ingots were homogenized in **Argon (A) or Helium (He)** (page 791). This is important metadata for heat treatment.
*   **Surface Preparation Details:** The text mentions "abraded to a final **3/0 emery paper**" and "pickled... to remove the cold worked surface." The specific temperature of the pickling bath (**80°C**) was captured in some entries but missed in others.
*   **Electrolyte details:** For NaBr tests, the absence of Chloride should be explicitly noted in the aggressive ions section.

### 5. Schema Recommendations for the Refiner Agent
1.  **Enforce Numeric Splitting:** Update the schema to strictly separate `value` (float) and `unit` (string) for: `test_temperature`, `step_potential`, `step_time`, `ion_concentration`, and `heat_treatment_temp`.
2.  **Step-wise Potentiostatic Schema:** Replace the flat `methodology` string with a nested object:
    *   `"test_protocol": {"step_size_mv": 50, "step_duration_minutes": 5, "steady_state_threshold_hours": 10}`.
3.  **Chemical Processing Structure:** Expand `pre_passivation_steps` from a string to an array of objects:
    *   `{"reagent": "HNO3", "concentration_value": 15, "concentration_unit": "%"}, {"reagent": "HF", "concentration_value": 5, "concentration_unit": "%"}`.
4.  **Gas Purification Metadata:** Add a field under `dissolved_oxygen` for `gas_purification_method` to capture the "passed over Cu at 450°C" detail, which is relevant for oxygen partial pressure in deaerated solutions.
5.  **Compositional Precision:** Ensure the "Balance" element is explicitly calculated or identified (e.g., Fe for stainless steels) and that trace elements like **Carbon** are included when provided in the text.
