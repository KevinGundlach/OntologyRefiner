
### 316L

#### Experiment: Potentiodynamic Polarization in Halide Solutions

* 
**Alloy Name/Identifier**: 316L.


* 
**Alloy Type**: Stainless steel.


* **Alloy Composition**: Not specified. The text mentions its composition is quite similar to type 316, but exact elemental percentages are not provided.


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: Cut from bar or sheet material.


* **Microstructure**: Not specified.
* **Heat Treatment**: Not specified.
* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: Cylindrical test samples machined and polished so that only the transverse surface was exposed to the electrolyte. The samples were mounted in a crevice-free electrode holder featuring a Teflon collar filled with argon gas.


* **Test Procedure & Environment**:
* 
**Methodology**: Potentiodynamic polarization.


* 
**Electrolyte**: NaCl and NaBr solutions.


* 
**Chloride/Halide Concentration**: Ranged from 0.1 M to ~2.0 M (estimated from logarithmic scale in Fig. 2a). Tests were also run at 0.5 M NaCl and 0.5 M NaBr.


* 
**pH**: Evaluated at pH 4 for variable concentration tests. Evaluated from pH 3 to pH 9 for the 0.5 M halide tests.


* **Temperature**: Not specified.
* 
**Aeration**: Oxygen content was reduced to a negligible value by bubbling nitrogen through the solutions for 24 h prior to testing, and the cell was purged with nitrogen during the experiment.


* 
**Scan Rate**: 60 mV $min^{-1}$.




* **Pitting Potential ($E_{p}$)**:
* ~230 mV to ~330 mV vs. SCE in NaCl solutions at pH 4 (estimated from Fig. 2a).


* ~290 mV to ~365 mV vs. SCE in NaBr solutions at pH 4 (estimated from Fig. 2a).


* ~255 mV (pH 3) to ~320 mV (pH 9) vs. SCE in 0.5 M NaCl (estimated from Fig. 2b).


* ~280 mV (pH 3) to ~335 mV (pH 9) vs. SCE in 0.5 M NaBr (estimated from Fig. 2b).




* **Other Relevant Metrics**: Not specified.

### 316

#### Experiment: Potentiodynamic and Potentiostatic Polarization of Wire Loops

* 
**Alloy Name/Identifier**: 316.


* 
**Alloy Type**: Stainless steel.


* 
**Alloy Composition**: Contains just over 2.0% Mo. Other elements not specified.


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: Wire material.


* 
**Microstructure**: Not specified, though the text notes the presence of flaws such as sulphides and inclusions at the surface.


* **Heat Treatment**: Not specified.
* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: 0.25 mm wire looped to a diameter of 25 mm. Immersed to half distance giving an exposed area of $0.32~cm^{2}$. Washed in distilled water and methanol, used in as-received state. Specific tests utilized chemical pre-treatments: immersion in 50% $HNO_{3}$ at 50°C for 30 min, or 20% $HNO_{3}$ + 4% HF at 65°C for 10 min.


* **Test Procedure & Environment**:
* 
**Methodology**: Potentiodynamic polarization and potentiostatic testing.


* 
**Electrolyte**: NaCl, NaBr, mixtures of NaCl and NaBr, and halide mixtures balanced with $Na_{2}SO_{4}$.


* 
**Chloride/Halide Concentration**: Ranged from 0.01 M to 1.0 M.


* 
**pH**: Tested at pH 2.5 , pH 3 , and pH 5.5.


* **Temperature**: Not specified.
* 
**Aeration**: Deaerated with nitrogen for 24 h and purged during testing.


* 
**Scan Rate**: 60 mV $min^{-1}$ for potentiodynamic tests.




* **Pitting Potential ($E_{p}$)**:
* 530 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaCl at pH 3.


* 330 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaBr at pH 3.


* ~550 mV to ~800 mV vs. SCE in NaCl at pH 5.5 (estimated from Fig. 4a).


* ~370 mV to ~510 mV vs. SCE in NaBr at pH 5.5 (estimated from Fig. 4a).


* 540 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaCl following 20% $HNO_{3}$ + 4% HF pre-treatment.


* 670 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaCl following 50% $HNO_{3}$ pre-treatment.


* 350 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaBr following 20% $HNO_{3}$ + 4% HF pre-treatment.


* 385 mV vs. SCE in 1.0 M NaBr following 50% $HNO_{3}$ pre-treatment.




* **Other Relevant Metrics**: Not specified.
