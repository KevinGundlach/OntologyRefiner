# Base Material
- identifier: Pure Nickel
- alloy composition: ~100 wt% Ni (Carbonyl nickel) [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- alloy type: Pure elemental metal [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- microstructure: Single-phase (implied for pure elemental metal) [Not explicitly specified in text]

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: Annealed Pure Nickel
- process_description: Vacuum melted in dense alumina crucibles. Drawn into 7 mm diameter Vycor tubes and water quenched. Cold rolled to 0.25 cm thickness. Electrodes (approx. 2 cm long, 0.5 cm wide by 0.2 cm thick) were abraded to 3/0 emery paper and pickled in 15% HNO3 + 5% HF at 80°C for 5 min to remove the cold-worked surface. [Page 1-2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- heat_treatment: Homogenized in Ar or He gas at 1050-1100°C for several hours. After cold rolling, annealed at 1000-1050°C and water quenched. [Page 1, Section: Experimental Procedure]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization. Electrode was first polarized at the least noble potential within the passive region for at least 15 min to achieve steady-state passivity. Potential was then advanced in stages of 50 mV, allowing 5 min between each change. Steady-state critical pitting potential ($V_c$) was obtained by holding the potential for long times (10 hours or longer) at a fixed value and observing for pitting. [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1N NaCl deaerated with purified nitrogen (passed over Cu at 450°C, without flow during measurements). Maintained in an air thermostat at 25°C ± 0.2°C. [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- pitting_potential: 
  - 0.28 V [Page 1, Table I]

# Base Material
- identifier: Binary Cr-Fe Alloys
- alloy composition: 12 to 57.8 wt% Cr, balance Fe (with 0.001-0.002 wt% C) [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure & Results]
- alloy type: Binary chromium-iron alloys [Page 1, Abstract]
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: Annealed Cr-Fe Alloys
- process_description: Vacuum melted from electrolytic grade iron and high-purity chromium in dense alumina crucibles. Drawn into 7 mm diameter Vycor tubes and water quenched. Lower % Cr alloys were swaged to ~0.45 cm diameter rods. Alloys >40% Cr were machined directly from homogenized ingots due to difficulty in rolling/swaging. Electrodes abraded to 3/0 emery paper and pickled in 15% HNO3 + 5% HF at 80°C for 5 min. [Page 1-2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- heat_treatment: Homogenized in Ar or He at 1050-1100°C for several hours. Swaged rods were annealed at 1050°C and water quenched. [Page 1, Section: Experimental Procedure]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization. Potential held for 15 min at passive potential, then advanced in 50 mV increments (5 min per step). Steady-state $V_c$ determined by holding for ≥10 hours. [Page 2, Section: Experimental Procedure]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1N NaCl deaerated with purified nitrogen, maintained at 25°C. [Page 2, Section: Results]
- pitting_potential: 
  - 12 wt% Cr: ~0.20 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 20 wt% Cr: ~0.22 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 25 wt% Cr: ~0.30 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 30 wt% Cr: ~0.65 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 35 wt% Cr: ~0.80 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 40 wt% Cr: ~0.90 V [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 48.8 wt% Cr: ~0.95 V (chromates only indicated at higher values) [Page 2, Fig. 1, estimated]
  - 57.8 wt% Cr: No pitting observed (reaches transpassive region/corrodes uniformly) [Page 2, Section: Results]
