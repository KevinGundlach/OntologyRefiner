# Base Material
- identifier: 316L Stainless Steel
- alloy composition: Not specified in text [Page 3, Text]. Mentioned to have chemical composition quite similar to steel type 316, particularly in relation to major alloying elements.
- alloy type: Austenitic stainless steel (implied by typical 316L designation, though text just describes it as "stainless steel type 316L").
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: 316L Bar Samples
- process_description: Machined and polished into cylindrical test samples. Only the transverse surface of the specimen is exposed to the electrolyte solution [Page 2, Text]. Mounted in a crevice-free electrode holder with a Teflon collar filled with argon gas to prevent crevice geometries [Page 2, Text & Fig 1a].
- heat_treatment: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiodynamic polarization. Test solutions were deaerated by bubbling nitrogen for 24 h. Electrochemical measurements performed in a 3-electrode glass cell purged with nitrogen. The sample was introduced at a cathodic potential of -800 mV(SCE) and held for 5 minutes to ensure a reproducible surface. Scan rate was 60 mV/min. Reference electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2-3, Experimental Method].
- test_solution_and_environment: NaCl and NaBr solutions of varying concentration (0.01 M to 1.0 M) at pH 4, and 0.5 M NaCl / 0.5 M NaBr solutions at varying pH (2 to 9) [Page 3, Fig 2]. Temperature not explicitly stated (assumed ambient).
- pitting_potential: 
  - In NaCl solutions (pH 4): Decreases from ~360 mV(SCE) to ~230 mV(SCE) as NaCl concentration increases from 0.1 M to 1.0 M [Estimated from Page 3, Fig 2a].
  - In NaBr solutions (pH 4): Decreases from ~370 mV(SCE) to ~290 mV(SCE) as NaBr concentration increases from 0.1 M to 1.0 M [Estimated from Page 3, Fig 2a].
  - In 0.5 M NaCl: Increases from ~260 mV(SCE) at pH 2 to ~325 mV(SCE) at pH 9 [Estimated from Page 3, Fig 2b].
  - In 0.5 M NaBr: Increases from ~280 mV(SCE) at pH 2 to ~340 mV(SCE) at pH 9 [Estimated from Page 3, Fig 2b].


# Base Material
- identifier: 316 Stainless Steel
- alloy composition: Contains "just over 2.0% Mo" [Page 9, Text]. Full composition not specified in text, but described as quite similar to 316L.
- alloy type: Austenitic stainless steel.
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: 316 Wire Loop (As-received)
- process_description: Made from 0.25 mm wire looped to a diameter of 25 mm (unless otherwise stated) yielding an exposed surface area of 0.32 cm^2. Washed in distilled water and methanol, used in the as-received state [Page 2, Experimental Method]. Attached to a specialized wire loop electrode holder [Page 2, Fig 1b].
- heat_treatment: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiodynamic polarization. Solutions deaerated by nitrogen bubbling for 24 h; cell N2 purged. Cathodic hold at -800 mV(SCE) for 5 minutes. Scan rate: 60 mV/min. Reference electrode: SCE. Results are averages of at least three identical experiments [Page 2-3, Experimental Method].
- test_solution_and_environment: Variety of tests: (1) 1.0 M NaCl and 1.0 M NaBr at pH 3. (2) Varying loop sizes in 1.0 M NaCl, pH 3. (3) Varying halide concentrations at pH 5.5. (4) Mixed NaCl/NaBr solutions (total 1.0 M) at pH 5.5.
- pitting_potential: 
  - 1.0 M NaCl (pH 3), 25 mm loop: 530 mV(SCE) [Page 5, Table 1 & Page 8, Table 2]. *Note: An individual plotted trace in Fig 3 marks a breakdown at 678 mV(SCE) for NaCl.*
  - 1.0 M NaCl (pH 3), 70 mm loop: 530 mV(SCE) [Page 5, Table 1].
  - 1.0 M NaCl (pH 3), 22 mm loop: 517 mV(SCE) [Page 5, Table 1].
  - 1.0 M NaCl (pH 3), 17 mm loop: 485 mV(SCE) [Page 5, Table 1].
  - 1.0 M NaBr (pH 3), 25 mm loop: 330 mV(SCE) [Page 8, Table 2]. *Note: An individual plotted trace in Fig 3 marks a breakdown at 185 mV(SCE) for NaBr.*
  - In NaCl solutions (pH 5.5): Decreases from ~800 mV(SCE) at 0.1 M to ~550 mV(SCE) at 1.0 M [Estimated from Page 5, Fig 4a].
  - In NaBr solutions (pH 5.5): Decreases from ~500 mV(SCE) at 0.1 M to ~380 mV(SCE) at 1.0 M [Estimated from Page 5, Fig 4a].
  - In 0.5 M NaCl (varying pH 2 to 9): Increases from ~550 mV(SCE) at pH 3 to ~630 mV(SCE) at pH 9 [Estimated from Page 5, Fig 4b].
  - In 0.5 M NaBr (varying pH 2 to 9): Increases from ~350 mV(SCE) at pH 3 to ~400 mV(SCE) at pH 9 [Estimated from Page 5, Fig 4b].
  - In Mixed Halide (NaCl + NaBr = 1.0 M, pH 5.5): Potential drops sharply from ~600 mV(SCE) in pure 1.0 M NaCl to ~400 mV(SCE) when just 0.2 M NaBr is substituted [Estimated from Page 6, Fig 6].
