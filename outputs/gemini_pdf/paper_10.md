# Base Material
- identifier: Alloy 5 [Page 3, Table I]
- alloy composition: 25.1 wt% Cr, 5.0 wt% Ni, 2.46 wt% Mo, 3.04 wt% Cu, 0.16 wt% N, 0.065 wt% C, Balance Fe [Page 3, Table I]
- alloy type: Ferritic-Austenitic (Duplex) Stainless Steel [Page 2, Title and Introduction]
- microstructure: Duplex (ferritic-austenitic) structure [Page 2, "Development of Improved Alloy"]

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: Alloy 5 (Cast and Water Quenched)
- process_description: Produced as a casting with a 76 mm thick section. [Page 2, "Results and Discussion"; Page 3, Table I]
- heat_treatment: Heated for 2 hours at 1120°C followed by water quenching (W.Q.). [Page 3, Table I]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization carried out according to the ASTM G1 Committee recommendations. Potentials were referenced to a saturated calomel electrode (SCE). [Page 3, "Corrosion characteristics"]
- test_solution_and_environment: 3% NaCl solution, N2-purged, at a temperature of 30°C. [Page 4, Fig. 3 caption]
- pitting_potential: ~ +1.0 V vs SCE (Estimated from Figure 3 polarization curve at the sharp inflection point where current density rapidly increases) [Page 4, Fig. 3]
