# Base Material
- identifier: 99.99% Al [Page 1, Experimental]
- alloy composition: 0.0039 wt% Cu, 0.0016 wt% Si, 0.0007 wt% Fe, 0.0005 wt% Mg, Balance Al (Mn and Cr not present) [Page 2, Table I]
- alloy type: High-purity aluminum [Page 1, Experimental]
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: As-prepared (Pickled) 99.99% Al
- process_description: Specimens were polished to 3/0 emery paper followed by pickling in 2N NaOH at 80°C for 5 min and then washing in distilled water. [Page 1, Experimental]
- heat_treatment: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization; electrode polarized anodically within the passive region for 5 min at -0.56V (SHE) to achieve steady-state. Then a step-by-step technique was applied by increasing the potential 50 mV every 5 min. Supplemented by longer time runs maintaining a given potential near the critical value for at least 12 hr to find the least noble value where no pits were observed under a low-power microscope. [Page 1, Experimental]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1M NaCl, deaerated by bubbling purified N2 previously passed over Cu turnings at 400°C. Tested at 25°C and 0°C. [Page 1, Experimental]
- pitting_potential: -0.40 V vs SHE at 25°C; -0.41 V vs SHE at 0°C [Page 2, Table I]. Approximately constant near -0.40 V from 0°C to 40°C [Page 2, Fig. 1].

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization with a step-by-step technique. Supplemented by steady-state holds for at least 12 hr. [Page 1, Experimental]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1M NaBr, deaerated with N2. Tested at 25°C and 0°C. [Page 1, Experimental; Page 2, Table I]
- pitting_potential: -0.29 V vs SHE at 25°C; -0.32 V vs SHE at 0°C [Page 2, Table I]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization with a step-by-step technique. Supplemented by steady-state holds for at least 12 hr. [Page 1, Experimental]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1M NaClO4, deaerated with N2. Tested at 25°C. [Page 1, Experimental; Page 2, Table I]
- pitting_potential: 0.0 V vs SHE at 25°C [Page 2, Table I]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Corrosion potential measurement (used to simulate critical pitting potential due to Cu++ providing cathodic sites) vs continuous polarization (V'c) and steady-state polarization (Vc). [Page 3, Effect of heavy metal ions]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1N NaCl containing 5 ppm Cu++, 25°C. [Page 3, Fig. 4]
- pitting_potential: V'c (continuous potentiostatic value) approx -0.37 V vs SHE [Page 3, Fig. 4 (estimated from plot) / Page 3 text]; Vc (steady state) approx -0.40 V vs SHE [Page 3, Fig. 4 (estimated from plot)]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Continuous polarization runs (50 mV/5 min) to determine nonsteady-state critical potentials. [Page 4, Effect of cations]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1N solutions of KCl, RbCl, CsCl, SrCl2, BaCl2, and LaCl3. [Page 4, Effect of cations]
- pitting_potential: For KCl, RbCl, SrCl2, LaCl3: Potentials are the same within less than 20 mV as for NaCl (approx -0.40 V vs SHE). For BaCl2 and CsCl: nonsteady-state values were about 70 mV more noble (approx -0.33 V vs SHE) [Page 4, Effect of cations].

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: Anodized 99.99% Al
- process_description: Anodized in 15% H2SO4 at 25 mA/cm2 for specific times to achieve oxide film thicknesses of 0.3 µm and 1.4 µm. Washed and sealed. [Page 4, Discussion]
- heat_treatment: Sealed in distilled water at 90°C for 0.5 hr. [Page 4, Discussion]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Steady-state critical potentials determined by potentiostatic polarization, as well as continuous polarization runs. [Page 4, Discussion]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1M NaCl [Page 4, Discussion]
- pitting_potential: -0.39 V vs SHE (steady-state value for both 0.3 and 1.4 µm thick films); -0.33 V vs SHE (nonsteady-state continuous polarization) [Page 4, Discussion]
