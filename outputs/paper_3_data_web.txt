
## Alloy W5

### Pitting Potential Measurement

* 
**Alloy Identifier**: W5.


* 
**Alloy Type**: Ferritic stainless steel.


* 
**Alloy Composition**: 21.50 wt% Cr, 2.90 wt% Mo, 1.26 wt% Si, 1.47 wt% Ni, 39 ppm C, 220 ppm O, 80 ppm S, 66 ppm N, 10 ppm P, Balance Fe (Actual-Bottom values).


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: The alloy was prepared by vacuum induction melting of 50 lb heats. It was cast into a heated graphite split mold. The slab was hot rolled to 0.100 inch sheet, water quenched, electropolished, and then cold rolled to a final thickness of 0.0625 inch.


* 
**Microstructure**: The as cold-rolled condition shows grains elongated in the rolling direction with deformation bands and sigma-phase or chi-phase precipitate stringers along prior hot rolled grain boundaries. Complete recrystallization with no precipitates occurred after a 1093°C (2000°F) heat treatment.


* 
**Heat Treatment**: Tested in multiple conditions: As rolled, 427°C (800°F) for 1 hr (chamber cool), and 1093°C (2000°F) for 1 hr (water quench).


* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: Flat coupons measuring 6 x 0.75 x 0.0625 inch were machined for testing. Specimens were pickled for 5 minutes in 10% HNO3 + 0.5% HF at 85°C prior to testing.


* 
**Methodology**: Potentiodynamic anodic polarization and scratch potential measurements.


* 
**Electrolyte**: Synthetic seawater.


* 
**Chloride/Halide Concentration**: Not specified beyond "synthetic seawater".


* 
**pH**: 7.2 +/- 0.2.


* 
**Temperature**: 90°C.


* 
**Aeration**: Deaerated (boiled and treated to remove dissolved oxygen).


* 
**Scan Rate**: 10 mV/min (for dynamic scans).


* 
**Pitting Potential (E_pit)**: As rolled: Escan = 662 mV vs SCE, Escr = 310 mV vs SCE. 800°F treated: Escan = 160-642 mV vs SCE, Escr = 300 mV vs SCE. 2000°F treated: Escan = 420 mV vs SCE, Escr = 350 mV vs SCE.


* **Other Relevant Metrics**: Not specified.

### Desalination Plant Exposure

* 
**Alloy Identifier**: W5.


* 
**Alloy Type**: Ferritic stainless steel.


* 
**Alloy Composition**: 21.50 wt% Cr, 2.90 wt% Mo, 1.26 wt% Si, 1.47 wt% Ni, Balance Fe.


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: Prepared by vacuum induction melting of 50 lb heats and rolled to 0.0625 inch sheet.


* 
**Microstructure**: Ranged from elongated grains with precipitates (as rolled) to fully recrystallized (2000°F anneal).


* 
**Heat Treatment**: As rolled, 800°F for 1 hr (chamber cool), and 2000°F for 1 hr (water quench).


* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: Flat test coupons (6 x 0.75 x 0.0625 inch) were held in Teflon supports to deliberately provide crevice conditions.


* 
**Methodology**: Direct field exposure in a multistage flash evaporator desalination plant.


* 
**Electrolyte**: Seawater.


* **Chloride/Halide Concentration**: Not specified.
* 
**pH**: 7.0.


* 
**Temperature**: 121°C (250°F).


* 
**Aeration**: Run 1 was deaerated (5 ppb dissolved O2), while Run 5 contained 200 ppb dissolved O2.


* **Scan Rate**: Not applicable (exposure test).
* **Pitting Potential (E_pit)**: Not applicable (exposure test).
* 
**Other Relevant Metrics**: Corrosion rates for the "As rolled" condition were 0.02 mpy in Run 1 (low oxygen) and 3 mpy in Run 5 (high oxygen).

## F-Series & Levitation Melted Alloys (F460-F475, Ternary F-Series)

Because the researchers generated dozens of 50-gram levitation melts (detailed in Tables 2, 3, and 5), they share identical processing methodologies and laboratory testing environments.

### Pitting Potential Measurement

* 
**Alloy Type**: High purity experimental ferritic stainless steels.


* **Alloy Composition**: Varies strictly by alloy identifier. For example, the ternary F314B is Fe-25wt%Cr, and the modified F475B is 70.97 wt% Fe, 19.73 wt% Cr, 4.70 wt% Mo, 2.04 wt% Si, 2.13 wt% Ni, 0.34 wt% Nb, 0.09 wt% Mn.


* 
**Alloy Description & Processing**: Prepared as 50 gm heats by levitation melting. Cast into flat slabs, ground, and annealed for 16 hours at 1200°C in an argon atmosphere. Hot rolled at 1090°C to 1/16 inch, sand blasted to remove oxide, given a final anneal, and spark cut into discs.


* 
**Microstructure**: Not explicitly detailed for each individual 50g melt, though general ferritic microstructures with varying susceptibilities to crevice/pitting were noted based on composition.


* 
**Heat Treatment**: The B-series alloys received a final heat treatment of 1 hour at 1049°C (1920°F) followed by water quenching.


* 
**Surface Preparation & Geometry**: Spark cut into 1/2 inch diameter discs.


* 
**Methodology**: Potentiodynamic anodic polarization and scratch potential measurements.


* 
**Electrolyte**: Synthetic seawater.


* **Chloride/Halide Concentration**: Not specified.
* 
**pH**: 7.2 +/- 0.2.


* 
**Temperature**: 90°C.


* 
**Aeration**: Deaerated.


* 
**Scan Rate**: 10 mV/min.


* 
**Pitting Potential (E_pit)**: A brief selection from Table 5:


* **F460B**: Escr = 145 mV vs SCE
* **F465B**: Escr = 170 mV vs SCE
* **F470B**: Escr = 272 mV vs SCE
* **F475B**: Escr = 298 mV vs SCE


* 
**Other Relevant Metrics**: Many of these alloys underwent severe 6-day crevice corrosion tests in oxygenated 10% ferric chloride at ambient temperature (21°C), with results showing higher Escr potentials generally correlated to higher resistance to crevice attack.
