# Base Material
- Material_Designation: Iron Aluminide, FA-61 [Page 2, Table 1]
- Chemical_Composition_and_Metrics: Fe-72%, Al-28% (at%) [Page 2, Table 1]
- Microstructural_Features: Intermetallic compound based on iron-aluminum (Fe3Al) stoichiometry. [Page 1, Introduction]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: FA-61 (DO3 heat treatment)
- manufacturing_process: Arc-melted from constituent elements in a water-cooled Cu crucible in an argon-backfilled chamber. Remelted six times for homogeneity, drop cast into a Cu mold to form ingots. Hot-forged 50% at 1,000°C, hot-rolled 50% at 800°C, and warm-rolled 70% at 650°C to a final thickness of 0.75 mm. [Page 2, Materials]
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Annealed in air for 1 h at 850°C (recrystallization) plus 7 days at 500°C (ordering) followed by cooling in air, producing a DO3-ordered structure. [Page 2, Materials]
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Mounted in Maraglas 655 epoxy and cured at 80°C for 24 h. Finished by grinding on 600-grit SiC paper. Interface painted with Amercoat 90 epoxy and cured at 70°C for 10 h. Metal surface hand-polished with cloth and 9-µm diamond paste to remove air-formed oxide. [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- exposed_surface_area: 1 cm2 [Page 2, Polarization Tests]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Cyclic potentiodynamic anodic polarization testing to evaluate pitting corrosion susceptibility under aerated conditions. [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- pitting_potential: ~320 mVSHE (Estimated from plot) [Page 5, Figure 4]
- electrolyte_temperature: Not specified in text (ambient conditions implied).
- polarization_scan_rate: 600 mV/h [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- reference_electrode: Saturated (KCl) silver-silver chloride reference electrode (196 mVSHE); potentials converted to Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE). [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- electrolyte_composition: Mild acid-chloride solution: 200 ppm Cl- (5.5 x 10^-3 M NaCl) and 6.25 x 10^-5 M H2SO4 in distilled water. [Page 2, Electrolyte]
- aeration_status: Aerated (sparged continuously with O2). [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- corrosion_potential: ~100 to 200 mVSHE (Estimated from plot) [Page 4, Figure 3]
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Allowed to stabilize with continuous O2 sparging until potential change was < 1 mV/min. [Page 2, Polarization Tests]
- counter_electrode_details: Not specified in text.
- electrolyte_ph: 4.0 [Page 2, Electrolyte]
- passive_current_density: ~1-10 µA/cm2 (Estimated from plot) [Page 4, Figure 3]
