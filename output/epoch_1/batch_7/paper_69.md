# Base Material
- Material_Designation: Pure Aluminum and Aluminum-Molybdenum (Al-Mo) Alloys [Page 1, Abstract; Page 2, Experimental Procedure]
- Chemical_Composition_and_Metrics: Pure Al and Al alloyed with various concentrations of Mo (up to ~15 at.%), including specifically tested compositions such as 2.8, 5.4, 5.8, 6.0, 7.1, 9.0, 10.4, and 13 a/o (atomic percent) Mo in solid solution [Page 2, Experimental Procedure; Page 3, Table II; Page 5, Figures 4, 5, 6]. Average Mo composition was determined by dissolving the alloy off the Si wafer and measuring weight loss and element concentration in the solvent [Page 2, Specimen preparation].
- Microstructural_Features: The pure Al film exhibited a grain size of approximately 0.25 µm, which was reduced to 0.12 µm with the addition of up to 6 a/o Mo. All alloys retained a single-phase face-centered cubic (fcc) lattice structure where Mo atoms substitutionally replaced Al atoms. There was no evidence of Mo-rich precipitates or Al-Mo intermetallic phase formation [Page 3, Alloy characterization].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: RF magnetron sputter-deposited Pure Al film [Page 2, Specimen preparation]
- manufacturing_process: Metal films were prepared by depositing an Al target onto a 5 cm diameter single-crystal silicon wafer using an RF magnetron sputter deposition process at a growth rate of 5 Å/s. The vacuum pressure in the chamber prior to sputtering was 10⁻⁷ torr. The deposited film thickness was between 0.8 and 1.0 µm [Page 2, Specimen preparation].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: The silicon wafer was cooled to near -196°C immediately after the start of sputter deposition to minimize heating. After deposition, the alloy temperature was allowed to slowly increase to ambient prior to removal from the vacuum chamber [Page 2, Specimen preparation].
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Samples were masked with lacquer and allowed to dry for 24 h. An air-formed passive oxide layer naturally formed upon atmospheric exposure. Select samples were rinsed in high purity water and dried in a nitrogen stream prior to XPS analysis [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements & Surface analysis].
- exposed_surface_area: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization testing of pure Al films in deaerated 0.1N KCl to evaluate the baseline localized pitting resistance and passive behavior [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- pitting_potential: ~ -700 mV vs. SCE (Estimated from Figures 1 and 6) [Page 2, Page 5].
- electrolyte_temperature: Not specified in text.
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.2 mV/s, scanned anodically or cathodically from the corrosion potential [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) held approximately 2 mm from the working electrode surface using a Luggin probe [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- electrolyte_composition: 0.1N KCl, prepared by mixing 500 ml distilled water with reagent-grade KCl [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- aeration_status: Degassed under vacuum and subsequently deaerated with high purity nitrogen [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- corrosion_potential: -1405 mV vs. SCE (Table II) [Page 4] (Also stated as -1450 mV vs. SCE in text) [Page 3, Electrochemical behavior].
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Held in solution until the change in the open-circuit potential was less than 1 mV over a 6 minute period [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum wire mesh with a surface area at least ten times that of the working electrode [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- electrolyte_ph: Adjusted to pH 8 using KOH [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- passive_current_density: ~ 10³ nA/cm² (1 µA/cm²) (Estimated from Figure 1) [Page 2].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: RF magnetron sputter-deposited Al-Mo alloy films (e.g., Al-2.8Mo, Al-5.4Mo, Al-6.0Mo, Al-10.4Mo, etc.) [Page 2, Specimen preparation; Page 3, Table II; Page 5, Figure 6]
- manufacturing_process: Metal films were prepared by co-depositing Al and Mo onto a 5 cm diameter single-crystal silicon wafer using an RF magnetron sputter deposition process at a rate of 5 Å/s. The vacuum pressure prior to sputtering was 10⁻⁷ torr. Film thickness ranged from 0.8 to 1.0 µm [Page 2, Specimen preparation].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: The silicon wafer was cooled to near -196°C during sputtering to minimize heating and suppress the formation of Mo-rich precipitates, successfully retaining the Mo in solid solution. Slowly returned to ambient temperature [Page 2, Specimen preparation; Page 3, Alloy characterization].
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Samples were masked with lacquer and allowed to dry for 24 h. Air-formed passive oxides naturally developed prior to immersion. Samples tested for XPS were rinsed in high purity water and dried in a nitrogen stream post-polarization [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements & Surface analysis].
- exposed_surface_area: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization of Al-Mo alloy films (varying from 2.8 a/o to ~13 a/o Mo) in deaerated 0.1N KCl to evaluate the effect of Mo solid solution concentration on localized pitting resistance [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- pitting_potential: Breakdown potential increased monotonically with Mo concentration, ranging from ~ -600 mV vs. SCE for 2.8 a/o Mo up to ~ 0 mV vs. SCE for ~13 a/o Mo (Estimated from Figure 6). Specifically for Al-6Mo, Eb is ~ -200 mV vs. SCE (Estimated from Figures 1 and 6) [Page 2, Page 5].
- electrolyte_temperature: Not specified in text.
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.2 mV/s [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- electrolyte_composition: 0.1N KCl [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- aeration_status: Degassed under vacuum and deaerated with high purity nitrogen [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- corrosion_potential: Varied based on Mo content: -1002 mV for 2.8 a/o Mo, -953 mV for 5.4 a/o Mo, -877 mV for 7.1 a/o Mo, and -883 mV for 10.4 a/o Mo (Table II) [Page 4].
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Held in solution until the change in the open-circuit potential was less than 1 mV over a 6 minute period [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum wire mesh with a surface area at least ten times that of the working electrode [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- electrolyte_ph: Adjusted to pH 8 using KOH [Page 2, Electrochemical polarization measurements].
- passive_current_density: ~ 3 × 10² nA/cm² for the Al-6Mo alloy (Estimated from Figure 1) [Page 2].
