# Base Material
- Material_Designation: 18Cr-5Ni-10Mn Stainless Steels [Page 1, Abstract]
- Chemical_Composition_and_Metrics: [Page 1, Table 1] Five steel variations were investigated based on nitrogen (N) content (all values in weight %):
  * Variant 1: 0.0185 C, 9.9 Mn, 0.44 Si, 0.027 P, 0.018 S, 18.1 Cr, 5.7 Ni, 0.17 N
  * Variant 2: 0.025 C, 9.6 Mn, 0.23 Si, 0.030 P, 0.018 S, 17.7 Cr, 5.6 Ni, 0.12 N
  * Variant 3: 0.024 C, 10.4 Mn, 0.22 Si, 0.027 P, 0.022 S, 17.4 Cr, 5.5 Ni, 0.07 N
  * Variant 4: 0.020 C, 10.2 Mn, 0.31 Si, 0.026 P, 0.024 S, 17.8 Cr, 5.2 Ni, 0.25 N
  * Variant 5: 0.020 C, 10.7 Mn, 0.32 Si, 0.029 P, 0.022 S, 18.2 Cr, 5.4 Ni, 0.35 N
- Microstructural_Features: [Page 2, Phase Composition; Page 4, Conclusions] In the hot-forged condition, steels containing 0.12%, 0.25%, and 0.35% N were fully austenitic. Steels containing 0.07% and 0.17% N contained austenite with a small amount of δ-ferrite. Small second-phase particles were present, identified as carbides (Me₂₃C₆ type) and carbonitrides (Me₂(C,N) type). Pure nitrides and σ-phase were not detected. Pit nucleation predominantly occurred at boundaries between the austenite matrix and precipitated particles (presumably carbides). Quenching homogenized the alloys, made two-phase boundaries less susceptible to etching, and coagulated the inclusions.
