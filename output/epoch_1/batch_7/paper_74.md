# Base Material
- Material_Designation: Fe-29Cr [Page 1, Title; Page 2, Table 1] - High-purity laboratory heat ferritic stainless steel.
- Chemical_Composition_and_Metrics: Cr: 28.93, Mo: 0.012, W: 0.011, Si: 0.41, C: 0.0010, N: 0.0009, Nb: 0.11, Fe: bal. (wt%) [Page 2, Table 1].
- Microstructural_Features: Composed of a single ferritic phase, containing extremely low carbon and nitrogen interstitial contents to allow examination of the inherent effect of W and Mo. [Page 2, Introduction].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Fe-29Cr (0 wt% Mo/W) Solution Annealed [Page 2-3, Experimental Method]
- manufacturing_process: High-purity alloying elements were vacuum arc-melted and cast in the form of a button. The casts were homogenized for 2 h at 1250 °C, and then hot-rolled into 5 mm thick plates. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Cold-rolled into 2 mm thick sheets and solution annealed for 20 min at 1050 °C, followed by water quenching. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Not specified in text (tested after water quenching). [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- exposed_surface_area: Not specified in text (results reported as current density). [Page 3, Experimental Method]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic anodic polarization test in an aggressive chloride environment to determine the pitting potential. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- pitting_potential: -590 mV (SCE) (Pitting corrosion occurred directly at Ecorr) [Page 3, Results and Discussion; Page 7, Fig 4].
- electrolyte_temperature: 80 °C [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.5 mV s^-1 [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_composition: 4 M MgCl2 solution [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- aeration_status: Deaerated by purging with high-purity nitrogen throughout the test, starting from 1 h before the test. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- corrosion_potential: -590 mV (SCE) [Page 3, Results and Discussion]
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Stable corrosion potential achieved after about 2 h immersion in the solution. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum counter electrode in a 1 L multinecked flask (ASTM G5) with a salt bridge and high-silica tip. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_ph: Not specified in text.
- passive_current_density: Not specified in text (Pitting occurred at Ecorr without establishing passivity). [Page 3, Results and Discussion]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic anodic polarization test in sulfuric acid solution to evaluate passivity parameters (Epp and Ic). [Page 3, Experimental Method; Page 5, Results]
- pitting_potential: Not specified in text (Transpassive breakdown evaluated rather than localized chloride pitting). [Page 9, Fig 6]
- electrolyte_temperature: 30 °C [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.5 mV s^-1 [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_composition: 20% H2SO4 solution [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- aeration_status: Deaerated by purging with high-purity nitrogen throughout the test, starting from 1 h before the test. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- corrosion_potential: Estimated from Fig 6 as ~ -450 mV (SCE). [Page 9, Fig 6]
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Stable corrosion potential achieved after about 2 h immersion in the solution. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum counter electrode in a 1 L multinecked flask (ASTM G5) with a salt bridge and high-silica tip. [Page 3, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_ph: Not specified in text.
- passive_current_density: Estimated from Fig 6 as ~ 10 µA cm^-2. [Page 9, Fig 6]
