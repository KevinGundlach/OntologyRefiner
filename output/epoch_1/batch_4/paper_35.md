# Base Material
- material_designation: Austenitic stainless steel, Fe-20Cr-20Ni-6Mo (Low Nitrogen variant) [Page 1, Abstract; Page 2, Table 1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: Fe (Balance), Cr 19.86 wt%, Ni 19.98 wt%, Mo 5.99 wt%, N 0.011 wt%, C 0.006 wt%, Si 0.08 wt%, Mn 0.20 wt%, P 0.010 wt%, S 21 ppm (0.0021 wt%), W 0.03 wt% [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Austenitic structure. Homogenized structure free of active pitting inclusions (SEM confirmed pits are formed on inclusion-free areas). [Page 2, Experimental Method; Page 4, Section: Experimental Results]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Fe-Cr-Ni-Mo (0.011 wt% N) [Page 2, Table 1]
- manufacturing_process: Not explicitly specified beyond being an alloy produced for the experiments (acknowledged as produced by Sandvik AB) [Page 18, Acknowledgements]. 
- heat_treatment_parameters: Heat treated at 1100 and 1250°C followed by quenching in water in order to homogenize the structure. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Mechanically polished on emery paper and finally on 1 µm diamond paste. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- exposed_surface_area: Samples were 11 mm in diameter ($\phi$ = 11 mm), corresponding to a geometric exposed surface area of approximately 0.95 cm². [Page 2, Experimental Method]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization in de-aerated acidic chloride solution at room temperature to evaluate fundamental passivation and pitting kinetics. [Page 2, Experimental Method; Page 3, Experimental Results]
- pitting_potential: > 800 mV (SCE). The alloy is passive up to the transpassive potential at room temperature; pitting potential is not distinctly reached before transpassive dissolution/oxygen evolution occurs (initiation and repassivation of pits occur instead of stable propagation). [Page 3, Fig. 1; Page 16, Discussion]
- electrolyte_temperature: 22°C [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- polarization_scan_rate: 1 mV s⁻¹ [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_composition: 0.1 M HCl + 0.4 M NaCl prepared with de-ionized water. Contaminants in water included <0.005 mg/L NO₃⁻, 0.08 mg/L NO₂⁻, and 0.03 mg/L NH₄⁺. [Page 2, Experimental Method; Page 3, Table 2]
- aeration_status: De-aerated by Ar gas bubbling for 2 hours prior to exposure. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- corrosion_potential: Approximately -250 mV (SCE) (Estimated from the zero-current crossing in potentiodynamic curve). [Page 3, Fig. 1]
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: No open-circuit rest period specified. Samples were instead cathodically activated at -700 mV (SCE) for 10 min prior to the anodic polarization sweep. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- counter_electrode_details: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiostatic polarization stepping to evaluate pit initiation, propagation, and repassivation behavior at elevated temperature. [Page 2, Experimental Method; Page 3, Experimental Results]
- pitting_potential: < 500 mV (SCE) at 65°C. At this applied potential, the material showed a tendency for passivation within the first 10 s, but thereafter exhibited a steadily increasing current due to the active propagation of pits. [Page 3, Experimental Results; Page 4, Fig. 2c]
- electrolyte_temperature: 65°C [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- polarization_scan_rate: Potential stepped directly from the activation potential (-700 mV) to 500 mV (SCE) and maintained for up to 2 hours. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- electrolyte_composition: 0.1 M HCl + 0.4 M NaCl prepared with de-ionized water. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- aeration_status: De-aerated by Ar gas bubbling for 2 hours prior to exposure. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- corrosion_potential: Not specified for this specific potentiostatic test.
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Samples were cathodically activated at -700 mV (SCE) for 10 min prior to stepping to the test potential. [Page 2, Experimental Method]
- counter_electrode_details: Not specified in text.
