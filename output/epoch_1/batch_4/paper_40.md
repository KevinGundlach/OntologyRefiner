# Base Material
- material_designation: 25% Cr duplex stainless steel UNS S32550 [Page 1, Abstract; Page 2, Materials]
- bulk_chemical_composition: Cr: 25.92, Ni: 5.9, Mo: 3.19, Cu: 1.62, N: 0.2, Mn: 0.96, Si: 0.47, C: 0.03, S: 0.007, P: 0.024 (wt%) [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Duplex microstructure consisting of ferrite and austenite phases. Ferrite content ranges from 47% to 64% depending on the annealing temperature, with both ferrite and austenite grain sizes increasing at higher temperatures. The ferrite phase is enriched in Cr and Mo and depleted in Ni and Cu, whereas the austenite phase is enriched in Ni and Cu. Pitting was observed to initiate preferentially in the ferrite phase or at the ferrite side of the ferrite-austenite boundary [Page 6, Experimental Results and Discussion; Page 9, Table 2].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: UNS S32550 annealed at 1020°C
- manufacturing_process: Alloy produced in a wrought form as a bar approximately 10 mm in diameter. The outer part of the bar was removed by machining to produce sample rods 8.5 mm in diameter [Page 2, Materials].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Solution treated for 2 hours at 1020°C, followed by water quenching [Page 2, Materials].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: The end face of each rod was carefully machined to give a smooth finish, wet ground to a 4000 mesh finish, and then degreased [Page 2, Materials].
- exposed_surface_area: The end face of the 8.5 mm diameter rod was wetted, corresponding to a calculated geometric surface area of approximately 0.567 cm² [Page 3, Pitting potential determination].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization sweep conducted in a glass three-electrode cell. The sample rod was supported on the lid of the cell such that only the end face touched the surface of the test solution to avoid crevices. Pitting potential was defined as the potential at which the current density exceeded 50 µA cm⁻² [Page 3, Pitting potential determination].
- pitting_potential: 322 mV (SCE) [Page 5, Experimental Results and Discussion]
- electrolyte_temperature: 65°C [Page 3, Pitting potential determination]
- polarization_scan_rate: 1 mV s⁻¹ [Page 3, Pitting potential determination]
- reference_electrode: Saturated calomel electrode (SCE) [Page 3, Pitting potential determination]
- electrolyte_composition: Hot 3.5% NaCl solution [Page 3, Pitting potential determination]
- aeration_status: Not specified in text.
- corrosion_potential: Estimated from plot as approximately -120 mV vs SCE [Page 6, Fig. 3].
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Not specified in text.
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum wire [Page 3, Pitting potential determination].
