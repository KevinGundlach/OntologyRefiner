# Base Material
- material_designation: Experimental high-nitrogen stainless steel (Alloy No. 30) [Page 2, Table 1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: 24.32 wt% Cr, 19.90 wt% Ni, 6.06 wt% Mo, 0.440 wt% N, 0.30 wt% Si, 0.018 wt% C, balance Fe [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Fully austenitic matrix containing some inclusions. Energy dispersive X-ray analysis (EDAX) revealed that the inclusions possess higher Mo and Cr, and lower Fe concentrations than the surrounding austenitic matrix. [Page 6, Microstructure; Page 8, Figures 7, 9 & 10]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Alloy 30
- manufacturing_process: Melted in a vacuum induction furnace using commercially pure grades. Fe, Ni, Mo, Mn, and ferrochrome were melted in vacuum, followed by bleeding nitrogen into the furnace and introducing Mn4N or CrN wrapped in iron foil. Cast ingots were soaked for 30 minutes, hot rolled at 1205 C or 1095 C, pickled in 10% HNO3 + 2% HF at 65 C to remove scale, and cold rolled to 2 mm thickness. [Page 1, Experimental Procedure]
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Annealed at 1065 C for 5 minutes, followed by water quenching. [Page 1, Experimental Procedure]
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Pickled in HNO3-HF solution at 65 C. For electrochemical testing, specimens were mounted in epoxy, ground to 600 grit, rinsed with distilled water, and the specimen/mount interface was covered with a thin film of lacquer. They were kept in a desiccator and cleaned with distilled water just prior to transferring to the cell. [Page 1, 2, Experimental Procedure]
- exposed_surface_area: Approximately 1 cm² [Page 2, Polarization Test]
