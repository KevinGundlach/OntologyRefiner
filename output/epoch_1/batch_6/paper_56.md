# Base Material
- material_designation: High purity polycrystalline aluminum foil [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: 99.999% Al [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- microstructural_properties: Polycrystalline with a grain size of approximately 10-80 µm in the short transverse direction. Grains were elongated in the rolling direction. [Page 3, Section 2.2]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Pure Al mb (micro-band electrode) [Page 3, Section 2.3]
- manufacturing_process: Aluminum foil manufactured to dimensions of 2.5 x 2.5 cm and 20 µm thick, then cut into ~1.4 mm x 2.5 cm x 20 µm segments. [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- thermal_treatment_parameters: As-received (no additional thermal treatment specified). [Page 3, Section 2.2]
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Mounted on end in Buehler Epoxide resin (exposing the foil cross-section). Wet polished through 600 grit SiC paper, followed by successive polishing to a 0.05 µm finish in alumina suspensions. [Page 3, Section 2.3]
- exposed_surface_area: ~2 x 10^-4 cm^2 (nominal 10^-4 cm^2) [Page 3, Section 2.3; Page 5, Table 1]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Anodic potentiodynamic polarization in a flat cell to determine pitting and repassivation potentials. [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- pitting_potential: -0.376 ± 0.113 V vs. SCE (Mean at 99% confidence level) [Page 5, Table 1]
- electrolyte_temperature: Not specified in text. [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.1 mV s^-1 in the anodic direction, with a reverse scan triggered at 0.05 mA. [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- reference_electrode: Saturated calomel electrode (SCE) [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- electrolyte_composition: 0.6 M (3.5 wt.%) NaCl prepared using deionized water (18 MΩ cm resistivity) and ACS certified reagents. [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- aeration_status: Deaerated with N2 gas [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- corrosion_potential: ~ -1.0 V vs. SCE (estimated from cumulative probability plot) [Page 6, Fig 4a]
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Open circuit potential was measured for approximately 1 h [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum mesh counter electrode [Page 4, Section 2.3]
- electrolyte_ph: Near-neutral [Page 1, Section 1.1]

# Base Material
- material_designation: Ternary Al-TM-RE metallic glass alloy / Al90Fe5Gd5 [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: 90 at.% Al, 5 at.% Fe, 5 at.% Gd (Purity of elements: Al and Gd 99.9%, Fe 99.98%) [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- microstructural_properties: As-spun condition is a wholly amorphous structure. Partial devitrification produces ~10 nm α-Al nanocrystals embedded in the amorphous matrix. Full crystallization yields 400-600 nm Al grains with equilibrium intermetallic phases at the grain boundaries. [Page 2, Section 1.2; Page 3, Section 2.1 & 2.2]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Amorphous Al90Fe5Gd5 mb (micro-band electrode) [Page 3, Section 2.3]
- manufacturing_process: Fabricated using a single roller melt technique (splat-cooled). Ingots were arc-melted under argon. Spun-melt ribbons were prepared in a helium atmosphere using a 0.20 m diameter copper wheel at ~45 m/s. Ribbon dimensions: 15-20 µm thick, 1-2 mm wide. [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- thermal_treatment_parameters: As-spun (wholly amorphous condition). [Page 3, Section 2.1]
- surface_preparation_and_treatment: Mounted on end in Buehler Epoxide resin. Wet polished through 600 grit SiC paper, followed by successive polishing to a 0.05 µm finish in alumina suspensions. [Page 3, Section 2.3]
- exposed_surface_area: 10^-4 cm^2 [Page 5, Table 1]
