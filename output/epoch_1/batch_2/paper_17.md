# Base Material
- material_identifier: AlCoCrCuFe
- chemical_composition: Al, Co, Cr, Cu, Fe (High entropy alloy, nominally near-equimolar) [Page 2, 3]
- material_classification: High Entropy Alloy (HEA)
- microstructural_features: Heterogeneous, multiphase microstructure. Consists of a Cu-rich face-centered cubic (fcc) phase located in the interdendritic regions and a body-centered cubic (bcc) phase (Cu-depleted matrix). Exhibits intense segregation of heavier elements. [Page 4, 5; Figure 2, 3]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: As-cast AlCoCrCuFe
- manufacturing_process_description: Arc melting (AM) in a reduced argon atmosphere. Melted on a water-cooled copper hearth starting with pure (99.99 wt-%) metal chunks. [Page 3]
- heat_treatment_parameters: Not specified in text (tested as-cast).
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Ground to a 2000 grit finish using SiC paper. [Page 3]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization testing conducted in a three-electrode flat cell using a quiescent 0.6 M NaCl solution. Open circuit potential (OCP) was measured for 30 minutes before testing. [Page 3]
- stable_pitting_potential: -130 ± 5 mVSCE [Page 6, Table 1]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 3]
- polarization_scan_rate: 1 mV s^-1 (scanned from -150 mVSCE vs OCP to 2.0 VSCE) [Page 3]
- electrolyte_temperature: 25 °C [Page 3]
