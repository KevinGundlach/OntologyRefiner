# Base Material
- material_designation: Type 304 stainless steel (UNS S30400), wrought austenitic stainless steel [Page 1, 3]
- bulk_chemical_composition: Cr: 18.1 wt%, Ni: 9.3 wt%, Mo: < 0.4 wt%, C: 0.065 wt%, N: -, S: < 0.017 wt%, P: 0.025 wt%, Mn: 1.1 wt%, Si: 0.63 wt%, Cu: < 0.1 wt%, Fe: Balance [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Wrought austenitic stainless steel [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Type 304 SS, solution-annealed
- manufacturing_process: Available in 2.5-mm-thick sheet form, cold-rolled to 1-mm thickness, and cut into rectangular coupons 25 mm by 15 mm by 1 mm [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Sealed in silica (SiO2) tubes under a helium (He) atmosphere, solution-annealed in a furnace at 1,100°C for 20 min, and quenched with water [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Mounted in epoxy resin, ground using 600-grit emery paper, and scratch-free samples were produced by diamond polishing. Specimen-mount interface covered with a thin film of lacquer to minimize crevice corrosion [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- exposed_surface_area: Approximately 3 cm² [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Anodic polarization behavior determined using potential step technique in 1 N HCl solution to evaluate pitting corrosion susceptibility [Page 2-3, EXPERIMENTAL, RESULTS].
- pitting_potential: Estimate: ~ +350 mV_SCE to +400 mV_SCE [Page 3, Figure 1].
- electrolyte_temperature: Room temperature / ambient temperature [Page 3, RESULTS].
- polarization_scan_rate: Potential increased in steps of 20 mV_SCE, with current readings recorded after waiting at each set potential for 1 min (effectively 20 mV/min) [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- reference_electrode: Saturated calomel electrode (SCE) [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- electrolyte_composition: 1 N HCl solution, prepared from double-distilled water and pure analytical reagent (AR) grade chemicals [Page 2-3, EXPERIMENTAL].
- aeration_status: Deaerated by bubbling purified hydrogen (H2) before and during each polarization experiment [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- corrosion_potential: Not specified in text; estimate ~ -350 mV_SCE [Page 3, Figure 1].
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Sample was kept immersed in the experimental solution for 5 min at open-circuit potential (OCP) [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
- counter_electrode_details: Two smooth platinum (Pt) electrodes used as auxiliary electrodes [Page 2, EXPERIMENTAL].
