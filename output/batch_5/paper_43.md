# Base Material
- material_designation: 2024 T351 aluminium alloy [Page 1, Abstract; Page 2, Section 1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: Al (base), Si 0.06 wt%, Fe 0.17 wt%, Cu 4.54 wt%, Mn 0.63 wt%, Mg 1.51 wt%, Cr 0.01 wt%, Zn 0.08 wt%, Ti 0.03 wt% [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Grain size ranges between 50 µm and 700 µm, with grains elongated in the rolling direction. The microstructure contains coarse intermetallic particles (up to 30 µm in size) of two types: Al2CuMg (S phase) and Al-Cu-Mn-Fe-containing particles (e.g., Al6(Cu,Fe,Mn), Al7Fe2Cu, or (Al,Cu)6Mn). The matrix contains needle-shaped Al2CuMg hardening precipitates (~100 nm) and homogeneously distributed rod-shaped Al20Mn3Cu2 dispersoids (~200 nm). A 500 nm dispersoid-free zone systematically surrounds all coarse intermetallic particles. Grain boundaries contain hardening Al2CuMg precipitates and larger needle-shaped Al2CuMg (S phase) particles (~100 nm) [Pages 3-5, Section 3.1].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: 2024 T351 alloy coupons [Page 2, Section 2.1]
- manufacturing_process: Supplied in the form of a 25 mm thick rolled plate. Specimens were prepared by cutting 10 × 10 mm² coupons from the alloy plates [Page 2, Section 2.1].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Not specified in text beyond the standard "T351" temper designation [Page 3, Section 3.1].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Specimen edges were covered with varnish, leaving a 100 mm² rolling plane area uncovered. The exposed surface was mechanically polished with 4000 grit SiC paper, followed by 1 µm diamond paste, and finally rinsed with distilled water [Page 2, Section 2.1].
- exposed_surface_area: 100 mm² [Page 2, Section 2.1]

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiokinetic polarization testing from a cathodic potential of −900 mV/SCE to a highest potential of −400 mV/SCE to evaluate localized corrosion susceptibility and specific phase breakdown [Page 2, Section 2.2].
- pitting_potential: Two breakdown potentials were measured. The first breakdown potential ($E_{b1}$), related to S phase particle dissolution, is close to −720 mV/SCE. The second breakdown potential ($E_{b2}$), where matrix breakdown, pitting, and intergranular corrosion developed, is close to −620 mV/SCE [Page 6, Section 3.2; Page 15, Section 3.4].
- electrolyte_temperature: Room temperature [Page 2, Section 2.2]
- polarization_scan_rate: 500 mV/h [Page 2, Section 2.2]
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2, Section 2.2]
- electrolyte_composition: 1 M NaCl solution (pH=6) [Page 2, Section 2.2]
- aeration_status: Naturally aerated [Page 2, Section 2.2]
- corrosion_potential: Not explicitly stated in text (estimated from the polarization curve to be approximately −740 mV/SCE) [Page 7, Figure 7].
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Not specified in text.
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum [Page 2, Section 2.2]
