- microstructural_properties: Verified to be fully austenitic via metallographic evaluation after final solution treatment [Page 2, Experimental]. Significant microstructural inhomogeneity/microsegregation was observed in some laboratory heats, notably M1 (Cr/Mo variations up to 0.7%/0.8% with ~100 µm periodicity) and A1 (Cr/Mo variations up to 2.5%/0.9%), which acted as preferential pitting initiation sites in depleted bands [Page 5-6, Microsegregation; Figure 7]. Series P materials exhibited higher inclusion contents (sulfides and oxides) due to elevated oxygen levels (~260 ppm) [Page 5, Nonmetallic Inclusions].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Series K and L (Alloys K1, K2, K3, L1, L2, L3) [Page 2, Table 1]
- manufacturing_process: Prepared as laboratory heats, then sequentially forged, hot-rolled, and cold-rolled to produce 3-mm (0.12-in.) strips [Page 2, Experimental].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Solution annealed at 1,100°C for 20 min to achieve a uniform structure, followed by water-quenching [Page 2, Experimental].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Wet-ground to 600-grit silicon carbide (SiC) at least 18 h prior to testing [Page 2, Experimental].
- exposed_surface_area: ~10 cm² (1.6 in.²), including the flat surface and edges of the test coupons [Page 3, Experimental].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiostatic testing to determine Critical Pitting Temperature (CPT) and Critical Crevice Temperature (CCT). Specimens were held at a constant applied potential while the temperature was increased in steps until an irreversible current increase indicated stable localized attack. Resulting CPT values: K1=32.0°C, K2=38.0°C, K3=42.0°C, L1=70.7°C, L2=78.7°C, L3=92.0°C. Resulting CCT values (via clamped metal-metal crevice): K1=14.8°C, K2=15.6°C, K3=18.4°C, L1=56.4°C, L2=60.4°C, L3=68.8°C [Page 2, Table 1; Page 2-3, Experimental].
- pitting_potential: Not specified in text. (The study measured critical temperatures under a constant applied potential of 600 mVSCE rather than conducting potentiodynamic scans to find the pitting potential) [Page 2, Experimental].
- electrolyte_temperature: Systematically stepped upward by 2°C every 15 min until stable pitting or crevice corrosion was observed [Page 2, Experimental].
- polarization_scan_rate: Not specified in text. (Potentiostatic step-temperature method applied) [Page 2, Experimental].
- reference_electrode: Saturated calomel electrode (SCE) [Page 2, Experimental].
- electrolyte_composition: 0.2 M NaCl solution [Page 2, Experimental].
- aeration_status: Deaerated [Page 2, Experimental].
- corrosion_potential: Not specified in text.
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Not specified in text. (However, specimens were ground at least 18 h before testing) [Page 2, Experimental].
- counter_electrode_details: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Series P (Alloys P1, P2, P3) [Page 2, Table 1]
- manufacturing_process: Produced via powder metallurgy technique involving nitriding of stainless steel powder, followed by consolidation and extrusion [Page 2, Experimental].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Extruded at 1,230°C, then annealed at 1,000°C for 20 min (P1), 1,250°C for 10 min (P2), or 1,250°C for 1.2 s (P3), followed by water-quenching [Page 2, Experimental].
