# Base Material
- material_designation: Fabricated Ni-Cr-Mo Alloys [Page 79]
- bulk_chemical_composition: 24 unique compositions fabricated for the study. Nickel (Ni) ranges from 54.35 to 81.45 wt.%, Chromium (Cr) ranges from 9.52 to 32.02 wt.%, and Molybdenum (Mo) ranges from 4.92 to 25.34 wt.% [Page 83, Table 3.1]. 
- microstructural_properties: Designed as solid solution alloys. Fabricated as homogeneous defect-free ingots. Very high Cr and high Mo alloys were observed to have an inhomogeneous two-phase microstructure with one phase possessing a higher Mo content than the other [Page 79; Page 85].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Homogenized Fabricated Ni-Cr-Mo Alloys [Page 79-80]
- manufacturing_process: Raw materials (Ni 99.99%, Cr 99.99%, Mo 99.95%) were weighed and melted together using vacuum arc melting. Re-melting procedures were performed as necessary to achieve homogeneous defect-free ingots [Page 79].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Two-stage homogenization heat treatment: 1204°C for 24 hours, followed by 1260°C for 42 hours, and concluding with water-quenching for fast cooling [Page 79].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Ground and polished with 1 micron diamond paste. Between consecutive polarization runs, samples were re-ground and re-polished to the same 1 micron finish [Page 80].
- exposed_surface_area: 0.004 to 0.05 cm² [Page 80].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization testing to determine the critical pitting potential and repassivation potential of the 24 fabricated Ni-Cr-Mo alloys in a neutral chloride environment at various temperatures [Page 80].
- pitting_potential: Varies significantly by composition and temperature. Alloys that did not show a distinct pitting potential prior to transpassivity were assigned a value of 600 mV. Pitting potentials (Ep) were mathematically modeled via multiple linear regression as follows: at 60°C, Ep (mV) = 11.14(wt.%Cr) + 5.47(wt.%Mo) + 214.09; at 75°C, Ep = 14.93(wt.%Cr) + 11.62(wt.%Mo) + 10.6; at 90°C, Ep = 18.45(wt.%Cr) + 10.96(wt.%Mo) - 71.21 [Page 84, Table 3.2].
- electrolyte_temperature: 45°C, 60°C, 75°C, and 90°C [Page 80].
- polarization_scan_rate: Forward and reverse scan rate of 2 mV/s [Page 80].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 80].
- electrolyte_composition: 0.5 M NaCl in water, neutral pH [Page 80].
- aeration_status: Not specified in text.
- corrosion_potential: Not specified in text (plotted dynamically in Figures 3.3, 3.5, 3.7 but exact tabular values are not explicitly stated).
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Not specified in text.
- counter_electrode_details: Graphite rods [Page 80].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Cyclic potentiodynamic polarization in a low pH, high-chloride and oxyanion mixture to examine the localized corrosion resistance (pitting and repassivation) of the alloys in aggressive acidified environments [Page 90].
- pitting_potential: Varies significantly by composition and temperature. Pitting potentials (Ep) were mathematically modeled via multiple linear regression as follows: at 60°C, Ep (mV) = 12.39(wt.%Cr) + 9.27(wt.%Mo) + 133.87; at 80°C, Ep = 13.58(wt.%Cr) + 19.82(wt.%Mo) - 105.57; at 95°C, Ep = 16.46(wt.%Cr) + 22.88(wt.%Mo) - 219.34 [Page 86, Table 3.4].
- electrolyte_temperature: 60°C, 80°C, and 95°C [Page 81].
- polarization_scan_rate: Forward and reverse scan rate of 2 mV/s [Page 80].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 80].
- electrolyte_composition: 5 M LiCl, 0.24 M Na2SO4, and 0.26 M NaNO3, adjusted to a pH of 2.75 using HCl [Page 80-81].
- aeration_status: Not specified in text.
- corrosion_potential: Not specified in text.
