# Base Material
- material_designation: Stahl A, 18/10 Chrom-Nickel-Stahl (Austenitic Cr-Ni Steel) [Page 1, Title; Page 2, Table 1]
- bulk_chemical_composition: 0.019 wt% C, 0.49 wt% Si, 1.01 wt% Mn, 0.017 wt% P, 0.007 wt% S, 18.60 wt% Cr, 11.05 wt% Ni, 0.03 wt% Mo, 0.022 wt% N [Page 2, Table 1]
- microstructural_properties: Fully austenitic matrix in the solution-annealed condition. In the tempered (sensitized) condition, the microstructure features intergranular precipitation of chromium-rich carbides ($M_{23}C_6$) at the grain boundaries, which locally depletes chromium and promotes intergranular attack [Page 4, Section 3.1]. 

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Stahl A (15 min 1050 °C/W) - Solution Annealed [Page 2, Section 2]
- manufacturing_process: Initially prepared as 3 mm thick rolled sheets. To achieve a uniform grain size, the sheets were cold-worked by 20% to a final thickness of 2.4 mm [Page 2, Section 2].
- thermal_treatment_parameters: Solution annealed for 15 minutes at 1050 °C, followed by water quenching (1050 °C/W) [Page 2, Section 2].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Pickled in a nitric acid-hydrochloric acid solution (Salpetersäure-Salzsäure-Beize), washed in water and acetone, and left air-passivated (luftpassiv) prior to testing [Page 2, Section 2]. 
- exposed_surface_area: Specimens were cut to dimensions of 15 x 15 mm. The explicitly exposed geometric area to the electrolyte during the electrochemical cell testing is not specified in the text [Page 2, Section 2].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic (potentiokinetic) anodic polarization to determine the critical pitting potential ($U_b$, Lochfraßpotential) [Page 3, Section 2; Page 4, Table 3].
- pitting_potential: 0.21, 0.26, and 0.19 $V_H$ (Standard Hydrogen Electrode) [Page 4, Table 3]
- electrolyte_temperature: 20 °C [Page 4, Table 3]
- polarization_scan_rate: 0.12 V/h [Page 4, Table 3]
- reference_electrode: Measured using a saturated calomel electrode (gesättigte Kalomelelektrode), with values mathematically converted and reported on the standard hydrogen electrode scale ($V_H$) [Page 3, Section 2].
- electrolyte_composition: 1 M NaCl aqueous solution (1 m Natriumchlorid-Lösung) [Page 3, Section 2]
- aeration_status: Deaerated; the solution was continuously purged with purified nitrogen gas (mit nachgereinigtem Stickstoff durchspült) [Page 3, Section 2].
- corrosion_potential: Not specified in text.
- ocp_stabilization_protocol: Not specified in text.
- counter_electrode_details: Platinum mesh electrode without a diaphragm (Platinnetzelektroden ohne Diaphragma) [Page 3, Section 2].
