# Base Material
- identifier: Pure Nickel
- alloy composition: 100 wt% Ni (Carbonyl nickel). [Experimental Procedure]
- alloy type: Pure metal. [Experimental Procedure]
- microstructure: Not specified in text. [Experimental Procedure]

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: Annealed Nickel
- process_description: Prepared by vacuum melting pure components in dense alumina crucibles. After allowing A or He to enter the furnace, the melt was drawn into 7 mm diam Vycor tubes and water quenched. Ingots were homogenized in A or He at 1050°C for several hours, cold rolled to 0.25 cm, and electrodes were machined to 2 cm long and 0.5 cm wide by 0.2 cm thick. Finally, abraded to 3/0 emery paper and pickled in 15% HNO3, 5% HF at 80°C for 5 min. [Experimental Procedure]
- heat_treatment: Annealed at 1000°C and water quenched. [Experimental Procedure]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Potentiostatic polarization. Electrode first polarized potentiostatically at the least noble potential within the passive region for at least 15 min to achieve steady-state passivity. Potential advanced in stages of 50 mV, allowing 5 min between each change. Steady-state critical potential (Vc) obtained by holding potential for 10-hr or longer and observing pitting under a low power microscope. [Experimental Procedure]
- test_solution_and_environment: 0.1N NaCl deaerated with purified nitrogen (passed over Cu at 450°C) without flow of nitrogen during measurements. Total immersion. Maintained at 25 ± 0.2°C. [Experimental Procedure]
- pitting_potential: 0.28 v [ABSTRACT] / 0.3 v [Results]
