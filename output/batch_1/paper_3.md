# Base Material
- identifier: F470
- alloy composition: Fe: 72.73 wt%, Cr: 21.48 wt%, Mo: 3.00 wt%, Si: 1.36 wt%, Ni: 1.43 wt%
- alloy type: High-purity ferritic stainless steel based on the ternary Fe-Cr-Mo system.
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: F470B
- process_description: Prepared by levitation melting as 50 gm heats to produce ingots in the form of flat slabs. Surfaces were ground and annealed for 16 hours at 1200 °C in an argon atmosphere. Slabs were hot rolled at 1090 °C to 1/16 inch thickness. Oxide scale was removed by sand blasting. Discs were spark cut from the resulting strips.
- heat_treatment: Final heat treatment of 1 hour at 1049 °C (1920 °F) followed by water quenching.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Conventional potentiodynamic anodic polarization at a scan rate of 10 mV/min to determine the pitting potential ($E_{scan}$) and determination of the scratch potential ($E_{scr}$) where the value is essentially independent of surface finish and polarization rate.
- test_solution_and_environment: Deaerated synthetic seawater at 90 °C. The solution was treated with sulfuric acid to neutralize bicarbonate alkalinity and subsequently boiled to remove free carbon dioxide and dissolved oxygen. The pH was adjusted to 7.2 ± 0.2 at 90 °C.
- pitting_potential: $E_{scr}$ = 272 mV vs SCE; $E_{scan}$ = 306 mV vs SCE. (Source: TABLE 5)
