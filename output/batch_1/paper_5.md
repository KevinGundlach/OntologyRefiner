# Base Material
- identifier: 825 (UNS N08825) [Page 4, Table 1]
- alloy composition: 30.0 wt% Fe, 2.2 wt% Cu, 42.0 wt% Ni, 21.5 wt% Cr, 3.0 wt% Mo, 0.9 wt% Ti [Page 4, Table 1]
- alloy type: Nickel alloy [Page 3, Introduction]
- microstructure: Not specified in text.

## Conditioned Material
- identifier: As-received sheet with 120-grit finish [Page 3, Procedure]
- process_description: Cut from commercially produced 0.062-in. (1.57-mm) and 0.125-in. (3.25-mm) sheet into 2 x 2 in. (51 x 51 mm) specimens. The specimens were fitted with multiple crevice devices produced by TFE-fluorocarbon washers. Before initial testing, all specimens were given a 120-grit surface finish with aluminum oxide paper and cleaned with acetone. [Page 3, Procedure]
- heat_treatment: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Materials Technology Institute (MTI) Procedure. Duplicate specimens were exposed for 24 h at a fixed temperature. If no attack was observed, the sample was considered immune at that temperature and was further exposed, without resanding, for another 24 h at a 2.5 °C higher temperature. This procedure was continued until crevice attack was observed to determine the Critical Crevice Temperature (CCT). [Page 3, Procedure]
- test_solution_and_environment: 6% FeCl3 (i.e., 10% FeCl3·6H2O) aqueous solution, prepared by dissolving reagent-grade FeCl3·6H2O into distilled water. Natural pH of 1.2 to 1.3 at room temperature. [Page 3, Procedure & Results]
- pitting_potential: Not specified in text. (Note: The study measures Critical Crevice Temperature, not pitting potential). [Page 4, Table 2]

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Modified MTI Procedure. Identical to the original MTI procedure, except the crevice corrosion specimens were resanded and cleaned before further exposure of the specimen at higher temperatures. [Page 3, Procedure]
- test_solution_and_environment: 6% FeCl3 (i.e., 10% FeCl3·6H2O) aqueous solution, prepared by dissolving reagent-grade FeCl3·6H2O into distilled water. Natural pH of 1.2 to 1.3 at room temperature. [Page 3, Procedure & Results]
- pitting_potential: Not specified in text. [Page 4, Table 2]
