## Conditioned Material
- identifier: MTI Procedure
- process_description: Crevice corrosion specimens were 2 x 2 in. (51 x 51 mm) by sheet thickness. Specimens were given a 120-grit surface finish with aluminum oxide paper and cleaned with acetone. Specimens were fitted with multiple crevice devices produced by TFE-fluorocarbon washers. (Source: Procedure)
- heat_treatment: Not specified in text.

### Experiment
- test_procedure: Materials Technology Institute (MTI) procedure from Manual No. 3 of May 1980. Duplicate specimens are exposed to the solution for 24 h at a fixed temperature. If no attack is observed, the sample is further exposed, without resanding, for another 24 h at a 2.5 °C higher temperature until attack is observed. The determined Critical Crevice Temperatures (CCT) were 0.0 °C and 0.0 °C. (Source: Procedure, Table 2)
- test_solution_and_environment: 6% FeCl3 (10% FeCl3·6H20) solution prepared with reagent-grade ferric chloride and distilled water. The solution has a natural pH of 1.2 to 1.3 at room temperature. (Source: Procedure, Results and Discussion)
- pitting_potential: Not specified in text.
