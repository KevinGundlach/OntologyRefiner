### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Increasing potential test at fixed temperatures in nitrogenated 3.5% (0.6M) NaCl (pH 3). The criterion for stable pitting initiation was defined as a rise in current density to 10 µA/cm² [Page 1, Figure 1 caption; Page 2, Effect of Testing Rate].
- stable_pitting_potential: Estimated critical pitting potentials from Figure 1: ~780 mV (at 10 °C), ~700 mV (at 20 °C), ~400 mV (at 30 °C), ~180 mV (at 50 °C) [Page 1, Figure 1].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 1, Figure 1 y-axis]
- polarization_scan_rate: Not explicitly specified in text (referred to as "increasing potential runs" or general "scan rates") [Page 2, Effect of Testing Rate].
- electrolyte_temperature: Conducted at discrete fixed temperatures ranging from 10 °C to 90 °C depending on the run [Page 1, Figure 1].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Increasing Temperature Screening Test. Samples freely corroded for 5 minutes, then potential was rapidly increased to +500 mV and held potentiostatically. Temperature was increased to find the Critical Pitting Temperature (T500) where current density rose to 10 µA/cm² in nitrogenated 3.5% (0.6M) NaCl (pH 3) [Page 1, Increasing Temperature Screening Test].
- stable_pitting_potential: Applied holding potential of +500 mV (SCE) [Page 1, Increasing Temperature Screening Test].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 1, Increasing Temperature Screening Test]
- polarization_scan_rate: Potentiostatic hold (no potential scan); temperature scan rate of 0.6 °C/minute [Page 1, Increasing Temperature Screening Test].
- electrolyte_temperature: Temperature was continuously increased. Pitting initiated at T500 = 30-32 °C (average 31 °C) [Page 2, Table 2].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Immersion exposure test in 10% (0.6M) FeCl3 solution. Bath temperature was increased by 2.5 °C increments and coupons were visually examined for pitting at each interval [Page 2, 3, FeCl3 Exposure Tests].
- stable_pitting_potential: Solution redox potential was measured at +695 mV (SCE) on bright platinum (corroding sample assumes a somewhat lower potential) [Page 2, FeCl3 Exposure Tests].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) [Page 2, FeCl3 Exposure Tests]
- polarization_scan_rate: Constant immersion (no applied potential scan); 24-hour holding duration per temperature step [Page 2, 3, FeCl3 Exposure Tests].
- electrolyte_temperature: Discrete step-wise increase. Pitting observed at 32.5 °C and above (No pitting at 30 °C and below) [Page 3, Table 3].
