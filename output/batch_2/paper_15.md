# Base Material
- material_identifier: Average Family of 18% Cr Austenitic Stainless Steels (Alloys 2-14, including commercial grades 304, 321, 316L, 317L) [Page 2, Table 1; Page 3, Figure 3]
- chemical_composition: Nominally 18% Cr, 9-20% Ni, 0.9-1.8% Mn, 0.5-0.7% Si, low Cu (NA to 0.02%), low N (0.03-0.08%), and varying Mo. Specific Mo (Wt%): Alloy 2 (NA/0), Alloy 3 (0.50), Alloy 4 (2.47), Alloy 5 (2.60), Alloy 6 (2.99), Alloy 7 (3.07), Alloy 8 (3.11), Alloy 9 (3.35), Alloy 10 (3.85), Alloy 11 (4.56), Alloy 12 (4.69), Alloy 13 (6.18), Alloy 14 (7.56) [Page 2, Table 1; Page 3, Effect of Molybdenum].
- material_classification: Wholly austenitic stainless steel [Page 1, Abstract; Page 3, Effect of Molybdenum]
- microstructural_features: Wholly austenitic (single phase); free of secondary phases (alpha or sigma) which are noted to limit pitting resistance [Page 5, Effect of Nickel].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Quench Annealed 18% Cr Average Family [Page 2, Testing]
- manufacturing_process_description: Experimental alloys were melted from virgin materials in 50 or 500-pound heats, deoxidized with Al (up to 2 pounds/ton), cropped, conditioned by grinding, and hot rolled to sheet. Commercial alloys were acquired via standard commercial practice [Page 2, Alloys].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Tested in the single phase quench annealed condition [Page 2, Testing].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Abraded with 120 grit silicon carbide paper [Page 2, Testing].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: 24-hour isothermal exposure tests in 10% FeCl3 solution to determine the Critical Pitting Temperature (CPT). The temperature was increased in 2.5 °C steps every 24 hours until discrete, macroscopic pits were observed. The baseline pitting resistance relationship for this family was determined to be: CPT (°C) = 5 + 7 × % Mo [Page 3, Testing & Results].
- stable_pitting_potential: Not specified in text (The study uses Critical Pitting Temperature as the primary criterion instead of Pitting Potential). However, for commercial 317L (Alloy 8), a referenced potentiodynamic curve (dashed line) shows the pitting potential boundary shifting from approximately +800 mV at 10 °C down to +250 mV at 90 °C [Page 1, Figure 1].
- reference_electrode: Saturated Calomel Electrode (SCE) (Used only to measure steady-state solution potentials during exposure: +660 mV for FeCl3 and +440 mV for K3Fe(CN)6) [Page 1, Effect of Potential on CPT].
- polarization_scan_rate: Not specified in text (Testing utilized go-no-go isothermal immersions rather than dynamic polarization scans) [Page 3, Testing].
- electrolyte_temperature: The test solution temperature was incremented in 2.5 °C steps. The measured CPT scaled linearly with Mo content, ranging from ~5 °C for 0% Mo up to ~58 °C for 7.56% Mo [Page 3, Testing & Figure 3].

# Base Material

- chemical_composition: Nominally 18% Cr, 12.3-24.27% Ni, 0.89-1.7% Mn, 0.43-0.63% Si, ~0.20% N, and varying Mo. Specific Mo (Wt%): Alloy 15 (2.42), Alloy 16 (2.83), Alloy 17 (3.04), Alloy 18 (3.20), Alloy 19 (3.55), Alloy 20 (4.48), Alloy 21 (5.60), Alloy 22 (6.0), Alloy 23 (7.30) [Page 2, Table 1].
- material_classification: Wholly austenitic stainless steel [Page 4, Effect of Nitrogen]
- microstructural_features: Single phase austenitic condition (the addition of nitrogen is noted to play an important role in stabilizing the single phase austenitic condition) [Page 4, Effect of Nitrogen].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Quench Annealed 0.2% Nitrogen Family [Page 2, Testing]
- manufacturing_process_description: Experimental alloys melted from virgin materials, deoxidized with Al, cropped, conditioned by grinding, and hot rolled to sheet [Page 2, Alloys].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Single phase quench annealed condition [Page 2, Testing].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Abraded with 120 grit silicon carbide paper [Page 2, Testing].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: 24-hour isothermal exposure tests in 10% FeCl3 solution to determine the CPT. The addition of nitrogen increased the resistance of the alloys by approximately 5 °C (9 °F) at every Mo level compared to the average family. The empirical relationship is: CPT (°C) = 10 + 7 × % Mo [Page 4, Effect of Nitrogen].
- stable_pitting_potential: Not specified in text (Study measures Critical Pitting Temperature instead).
- reference_electrode: Not specified in text.
- polarization_scan_rate: Not specified in text.
- electrolyte_temperature: The test solution temperature was incremented in 2.5 °C steps. The measured CPT ranged from ~27 °C for 2.42% Mo to ~61 °C for 7.30% Mo [Page 3, Testing; Page 4, Figure 4].

# Base Material

- chemical_composition: 17.3-18.8% Cr, 20.17-24.86% Ni, 1.0-1.72% Mn, 0.44-0.53% Si, ~0.03-0.06% N, and varying Cu and Mo. Specific Mo/Cu (Wt%): Alloy 24 (3.06 Mo, 1.92 Cu), Alloy 25 (4.43 Mo, 1.9 Cu), Alloy 26 (4.33 Mo, 1.32 Cu), Alloy 27 (4.60 Mo, residual/NA Cu) [Page 2, Table 1].
- material_classification: Wholly austenitic stainless steel [Page 1, Abstract]
- microstructural_features: Single phase austenitic [Page 2, Testing]

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Quench Annealed Copper-Alloyed Family [Page 2, Testing]
- manufacturing_process_description: Experimental and commercial melts, hot rolled to sheet [Page 2, Alloys].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Single phase quench annealed condition [Page 2, Testing].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Abraded with 120 grit silicon carbide paper [Page 2, Testing].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: 24-hour isothermal exposure tests in 10% FeCl3 solution to determine CPT. The addition of ~2% Cu had no measurable effect on pit initiation resistance. Alloys 24 and 25 matched the average family (CPT = 5 + 7 × % Mo), while Alloys 26 and 27 matched the nitrogen family (CPT = 10 + 7 × % Mo) [Page 4, Effect of Copper].
- stable_pitting_potential: Not specified in text.
- reference_electrode: Not specified in text.
- polarization_scan_rate: Not specified in text.
- electrolyte_temperature: The test solution temperature was incremented in 2.5 °C steps. The measured CPT was ~26 °C for Alloy 24, ~36 °C for Alloys 25 and 26, and ~38 °C for Alloy 27 [Page 4, Figure 5].

# Base Material
- material_identifier: Low Manganese (<0.5% Mn) Family of 18% Cr Austenitic Stainless Steels (Alloys 31-36) [Page 2, Table 1; Page 4, Effect of Manganese]
- chemical_composition: 17.5-19.38% Cr, 12.5-23.8% Ni, 0.38-0.53% Mn (low Mn), 0.33-0.71% Si, 0.03-0.23% N, and varying Mo. Specific Mo (Wt%): Alloy 31 (2.50), Alloy 32 (2.75), Alloy 33 (2.85), Alloy 34 (3.26), Alloy 35 (6.04), Alloy 36 (6.30) [Page 2, Table 1].
- material_classification: Wholly austenitic stainless steel [Page 1, Abstract]
- microstructural_features: Single phase austenitic. The enhanced pitting resistance compared to commercial grades is attributed to the presence of "different sulfide phases in equilibrium with alloys of different Mn content" [Page 5, Summary of Results].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: Quench Annealed Low Manganese Family [Page 2, Testing]
- manufacturing_process_description: Experimental melts, deoxidized with Al, hot rolled to sheet [Page 2, Alloys].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Single phase quench annealed condition [Page 2, Testing].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Abraded with 120 grit silicon carbide paper [Page 2, Testing].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: 24-hour isothermal exposure tests in 10% FeCl3 solution to determine CPT. A large, synergistic improvement in pit initiation resistance was observed when low Mn was combined with increasing Mo. The empirical relationship is: CPT (°C) = 5 + 11 × % Mo [Page 4-5, Effect of Manganese].
- stable_pitting_potential: Not specified in text.
- reference_electrode: Not specified in text.
- polarization_scan_rate: Not specified in text.
- electrolyte_temperature: The test solution temperature was incremented in 2.5 °C steps. The measured CPT was significantly higher than other families, ranging from ~32 °C for Alloy 31 (2.50% Mo) up to ~74 °C for Alloy 36 (6.30% Mo) [Page 5, Figure 6].
