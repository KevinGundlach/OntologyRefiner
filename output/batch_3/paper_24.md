# Base Material
- material_designation_and_classification: Co1.5CrFeNi1.5Ti0.5Mo0 high-entropy alloy, designated as Mo0. Multiprincipal-element high-entropy alloy (HEA) system [Page 2, Table 1; Page 2, Section 2.1].
- chemical_composition: 28.68 wt% Co, 16.87 wt% Cr, 18.12 wt% Fe, 28.56 wt% Ni, 7.77 wt% Ti, 0 wt% Mo [Page 2, Table 1].
- microstructural_properties: Simple FCC solid-solution structure. The as-cast microstructure is composed of dendrites (darker phase, Fe and Cr rich) and interdendrites (lighter phase, Ni and Ti rich). No sigma ($\sigma$) phase was observed [Page 3, Section 3.1; Page 4, Table 2].

## Conditioned Material
- material_identifier: As-cast Mo0 alloy specimen [Page 2, Section 2.1].
- manufacturing_process: High-purity elements (>99 wt%) and a Fe-Mo master alloy were melted in an induction furnace at 1600 °C after purging with argon. Co, Fe, and Ni were melted first, followed by the addition of Ti, Cr, and FeMo. The melt was poured into a shell mold preheated at 1100 °C to form ingots (8 × 7 × 14 cm). Test cylinders were cut by electric arc line into pieces 3 mm thick and 8 mm in diameter [Page 2, Section 2.1].
- heat_treatment_parameters: Not specified in text (tested in the as-cast condition) [Page 2, Section 2.1].
- surface_preparation_and_finish: Cold mounted using epoxy resin to yield an exposed surface area of 0.5 cm². Mechanically ground using a series of 240–600 grit silicon carbide (SiC) papers, then cleaned with acetone and distilled water. Prior to testing, specimens were cathodically polarized at -192 $mV_{SHE}$ for 5 minutes to reduce existing surface films [Page 2, Sections 2.1 & 2.3].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization testing in an acidic environment to evaluate active-passive corrosion behavior [Page 2, Section 2.3; Page 3, Section 3.2].
- pitting_potential: No pitting was observed. Breakdown potential ($E_b$) = 1089 $mV_{SHE}$ [Page 5, Table 4; Page 9, Section 5].
- electrolyte_temperature: 25 °C [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- polarization_scan_rate: Continuous scan at 1 mV/s from -0.5 V up to 1.5 V versus the open circuit potential (OCP) [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- reference_electrode: Silver/silver chloride (Ag/AgCl) in 3 M KCl. Note: All potentials in the study were converted and reported relative to the Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE) [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- electrolyte_composition: 0.5 M sulfuric acid ($H_2SO_4$) solution [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- aeration_status: Deaerated by bubbling purified nitrogen gas before and throughout the electrochemical tests [Page 2, Section 2.3].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Potentiodynamic polarization testing in a basic (alkaline) environment to evaluate active-passive corrosion behavior [Page 2, Section 2.3; Page 5, Section 3.2].
- pitting_potential: No pitting was observed. Breakdown potential ($E_b$) = 653 $mV_{SHE}$ [Page 6, Table 5; Page 9, Section 5].
- electrolyte_temperature: 25 °C [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- polarization_scan_rate: Continuous scan at 1 mV/s from -0.5 V up to 1.5 V versus the OCP [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- reference_electrode: Silver/silver chloride (Ag/AgCl) in 3 M KCl, reported vs. SHE [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- electrolyte_composition: 1 M sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- aeration_status: Deaerated by bubbling purified nitrogen gas before and throughout the electrochemical tests [Page 2, Section 2.3].

### Experiment
- experimental_test_summary: Cyclic potentiodynamic polarization testing in a marine-simulated environment to determine susceptibility to localized pitting corrosion [Page 2, Section 2.3; Page 6, Section 3.3].
- pitting_potential: Pitting potential / Breakdown potential ($E_b$) = 338 $mV_{SHE}$. The curve exhibited positive hysteresis, and SEM confirmed pitting corrosion had occurred [Page 7, Table 6; Page 9, Section 5].
- electrolyte_temperature: 25 °C [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- polarization_scan_rate: 1 mV/s. Scan began at -0.5 V, continued in the anodic direction to 1.5 V versus OCP, then reversed and returned to the starting potential [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- reference_electrode: Silver/silver chloride (Ag/AgCl) in 3 M KCl, reported vs. SHE [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- electrolyte_composition: 1 M sodium chloride (NaCl) solution [Page 2, Section 2.3].
- aeration_status: Deaerated by bubbling purified nitrogen gas before and throughout the electrochemical tests [Page 2, Section 2.3].
