## THE ROLE OF  MOLYBDENUM ADDITIONS TO AUSTENITIC STAINLESS STEELS IN THE INHIBITION OF PITTING IN ACID CHLORIDE SOLUTIONS*

K. SUGIMOTO and Y. SAWADA

Department  of  Metallurgy, Faculty of  Engineering, Tohoku University, Sendal, Japan

Abstract--In order  to clarify the  mechanism for  increased resistance to pitting in acid  chloride solutions by addition of  Me  to  Cr-Ni  stainless steels, the  anodic  polarization curves, a.c. electrode impedances, ellipsometric parameters and X-ray  photo-electron spectra have  been  measured  on the Me-containing steels passivated in IN HCI. The results showed that  the  presence of  an adequate amount of  Cr is  indispensable for  the  improvement  of  pitting resistance by the  Me  addition. The passive films  of  the  Me-containing steels were found to  be composed of  a complex oxyhydroxide containing Cr  a+  Fe  s+, Ni  s+, Me s+  and CI-  and  showed a  rather higher d.c. resistance in  HCI solution than  in  HsSO, solution. The thickness of  the  passive film  increases with  increase in  Me  content.

## INTRODUCTION

IT  HAS  long  been  known that additions of  Me  to  austenitic Cr-Ni  stainless steels, to  a marked  degree,  improve resistance to  chloride pitting. I-ls The  mechanism  by which the  increased resistance is  obtained is  not  well  understood. According  to  the literature, the  following propositions have  been  made as  to  the  effect of  alloyed Me: (1)  thickening of  the  passive films  of  the  steels, 14  (2)  forming  a heteropoly acidt film which is  less  readily hydrolysed and more readily polymerized) 6 (3)  impeding  the adsorption of  CI-  on  to  the  surface of  the  steel by  increasing the  oxygen  affinity of  the steel, s  and (4)  forming  an amorphous oxide  film  with  a glassy structure, is  Each of these effects is thought to  be  important and  has  to be  taken  into account in  elucidating the  mechanism for  the  improvement  of  pitting resistance by alloying with  Me.  In addition, however,  the  electrochemical behaviour  of  Me  in  the  potential region  of passivity for  Me-containing  Cr-Ni steels must also  be an important  factor to be considered.

In  previous work,  it has  been  reported that  alloyed Me  in  Cr-Ni stainless steels causes  the  transpassive reaction in the  potential range where the  Me-containing Cr-Ni  steels passivate. 17  A theoretical examination of  thermodynamic  data shows  that the  product of  the  transpassive reaction of  Me  depends  on the  pH  of  test solutions; that  is, MoOs  is  formed  in  solutions with  pH  &lt; ca. 3.5  and MoO~-  (or  HMoOT~)  in solutions with  pH  &gt; ca. 3.5Y s  Consequently, alloyed Me  in  Cr-Ni stainless steels will  be  retained in  the  passive films  as  a Mo(VI) oxide  or  dissolve into  solutions as MoO4  2-  according to  the  pH  of  the  solutions used  when the  Me-containing Cr-Ni steels are  polarized up to  the  potentials at  which  t ~ s i v a t e .

tA heteropoly acid  is an acid  in  which  some or  all of  the  oxygen  atoms  in  the  radical have  been replaced by a different acid  radical.

Since  MoO  2- can serve  as a very  effective pitting inhibitor, pitting of  the  Mocontaining steels in  neutral chloride solutions may be inhibited by MoO  2-  ions  which are  formed  by  dissolution of  M o   in  the  steels, xT,t9 In  the  case  of  acid  chloride solutions in  which  MoO  2-  ions  are  thermodynamically unstable, an increase in  pitting resistance by the  addition of  Mo  to  the  steels should  be brought  about  by the  improvement of properties of  the  passive films by Mo(VI) oxide  present in  the  films.

The purpose  of  the  present investigation is  to  clarify the  origin of  increased pitting resistance with  increasing Mo  content in  acid  chloride solutions by examination of  the composition  and characteristics of  the  passive films  formed in  these  solutions. For this  purpose,  measurements of  anodic  polarization curves  in HCI  solution and, in some cases,  in HsS04  solution, were performed on the Cr-Ni stainless steels with varying  amounts of  Mo,  several pure  components of  the  steels, and combinations  of the  two  alloying components.  On the  basis of  the  results of  these polarization measurements,  subsequent experiments were carried out  to  determine the  critical composition at  which Mo makes  the steels  highly  resistant to pitting. Measurements of a.c. impedance,  ellipsometric parameters, and X-ray photo-electron spectra  were  then conducted  on the  passive films  formed on the  surfaces of  the  Mo-containing  Cr-Ni steels in  order to  investigate how the  electrical properties, thickness, optical constants, and chemical  composition of  the  films change  with  the  Mo  content of  the  steels. On the basis  of  these  experimental results, the  role  of  alloyed  Mo  in the  steels in the inhibition of  pitting in  acid  chloride solutions is  considered and discussed.

## Electrolytic solutions

1N HCI (pH = 0.02) was  mainly used  as  the test solution and,  in some  cases, 1N HsSO4 (pH = 0.4) was used for  comparison. All  solutions used were de-aerated by bubbling  purified nitrogen for more than  30  rain.

## Electrochemical polarization measurements

In  the  measurement of  anodic  polarization curves, the  electrode potential was regulated by a potentiostat and current recording was made after holding the  potential for  1  rain at  each  setting of the  potential which was increased successively in  steps of  25 mV  from  the  corrosion .potential. The reference electrode, to  which  all potentials are  referred, was a saturated calomel electrode. Measurements were carried out  in  de-oxygenated  solutions at  room temperature (25°C)  under  stationary conditions.

## A.C. impedance measurements

The a.c. electrode impedances  of  various stainless steels were  measured  at a  fixed potential in  the passive region by using an  apparatus for measuring  Lissajou's ellipse, coupled to  a  potentiostat or  a galvanostat. 2°The applied a.c. amplitude was held  at ca. 5  inV.  For  the  frequency range  104-1  Hz, Lissajou's figures were  recored on the  Braun  tube  of  a  synchroscope and,  for the  range  1-10 -s  Hz, on  an  X-Y chart recorder. The series capacitive reactance, l/o)C,, and the  series resistance, R,,  were obtained by analysis of  the  resulting ellipse and the  complex  impedance, Z =  Ro  --  j/oC,, was plotted at  each  potential, It  is  clear that  the  resistance at  zero  frequency, R®~0, of  the  complex impedance  diagram  is the  d.c. resistance of  the  passive film, which is  thought to  be  directly proportional to  the  corrosion resistance of  the  film. 2x

## Ellipsometric measurements

To determine the  thickness and optical constants of  passive films formed  on the  Mo-containing 20Cr-25Ni steels, the  ellipsometric measurements were carded  out  on the  steel surfaces during anodie  polarization in  solutions. The  electrochemical-optical e l had  the same shape  as  that used  by Sato'et al. 2s and two optical windows whose angle  of  incidence was 60*. A  Mizojiri Kogaku type MD-36M ellipsometer was used  for the  measurements.  Ellipsometric parameters, A and ¥,  at  the extinction point were  measured after specimens  had been  held  at  each  potential for  1  h.  The wavelength of  the  monochromatic  light used for  the  measurement  was 5461 A, its angle  of  incidence being  60.00 °. The  film thickness and  the  optical constants were  calculated from  the values of  A and  ¥ with  the aid  of a  NEAC 2200  MOD VII  computer  on  the  basis of  exact Drude's equations. 2a

## XPS  measurements

In  order to  investigate the  kinds  and  valencies of  the  constituent atoms  of  passive films formed on specimens  in  HCI solution, the  surfaces of  specimens  treated at  various potentials for 1  h  were analyzed by  means of XPS using a  Kokusai  Denki  type KES-X-2001 electron spectrometer. The  specimen  container was evacuated to  10 -T  Torr  and  held  at  r.t. ## DISCUSSION

Measurements of the anodic  polarization curves in 1N  HCI  for  the Mecontaining Cr-Ni stainless steels with  various compositions lead  to  the  conclusion that additions of  Me  improve  the  pitting resistance of  the  steels in  the  presence  of Cr above  a certain level which  depends  on the  amount  of  Me  added.  Me  decreases the  corrosion resistance of  the  passive films when added  to  iron base  alloys without Cr.  Therefore, in  order to  clarify the  mechanism  for the improvement  of the  resistance to  pitting by  alloyed Me, it must  be  explained why the  existence of  Cr  in  steels is indispensable for  deriving the  beneficial effect of  the  Me  addition, and in  particular, why the  transpassive dissolution of  Me  in  the  passive films  is  suppressed  by the presence of  Cr  in  the films of  the steels. Since  this type of  improvement was  thought to be  concerned with  the  composition and  structure of  the passive films, a  consideration was given  at  first to  the  chemical  composition of  the  passive films formed  on the 20Cr-25Ni-SMo steel having  a  high  corrosion resistance in  1N HCI.

·  According  to  the  XPS  studies, the  passive film  of  the  20Cr-25Ni-5Mo steel is considered to  be an oxyhydroxide which contains Fe  3+,  Cr 8+,  Ni  ~+ and Me 6+ as cations. Among  these  ions, Cr  3÷ and Me 6+ should  play  a very  important role  in improving the  corrosion resistance of  the  films. The corrosion resistance of  the  steels should  increase when films which  arc  mainly composed of  Cr(III) oxyhydroxide and Mo(VI) oxide  are  formed  on  the  surfaces of  the  steels. It must  be  remembered,  however, that Mo(VI) oxide, e.g. MoOs, cannot form  by  itself a  compact  surface film having a  good  resistance to  corrosion, as  can  be  seen  from  Fig. 2,  which  shows  that pure  Me dissolves with  a large  rate producing  an unprotective oxide  film  of  MoO3 in  the potential range  above ±  0 V. The observed  phenomenon that the  current density in  the  passive state of  the  Fe-Mo binary alloy increases with  increase in  Me  content is  thought to  result from  the  nature of  the  MoOa oxide. On the  other hand,  Cr(III) oxyhydroxide, e.g.  CrOOH,  can compose very  compact surface films  which are stable to  the  attack of  CI-  and  acids as  can  be  seen  from  the  fact that pure  Cr  is  not subject to  pitting in  1N HCI. Therefore, the  formation of  a surface film  which is mainly  composed of  a Cr(III) oxyhydroxide should  result in  increased resistance to pitting as  in the  case  of  Fc-Cr  alloys with  high  Cr  contents.

## SUMMARY

In  order to  explain the  reason that the  addition of  Me to  the Cr-Ni  stainless steels results in  improved  resistance to  pitting in  acid  solutions containing CI-,  the  pitting inhibition of  the  passive films have  been  examined  by measurements  of  the  anodic polarization curves, a.c. electrode impedances, ellipsometric parameters and X-ray photoelectron spectra of  the  steels, with  varying Me content, passivated in  1N HC1.

- (1)  The presence  of  a certain amount of  Cr is  essential to  increase the  pitting resistance of  steels in  acid chloride solutions by  Me  alloying.
- (2)  The passive films of  the  Me-containing Cr-Ni  steels consist of  complex  oxyhydroxide containing Cr  3+,  Fe z+,  Ni  2+ and Me 6+.  The Me  content of  the  films
3. increases almost linearly with  increasing the  Me  content of  the  steels.
- (3)  Compact  surface films which  are composed  of Cr(III) oxyhydroxide containing a  relatively arge amount  of  Fe 3+  and  small amount  of  Me 6+  and  a  trace amount of Ni  z+  may be  formed  on  the  20Cr-25Ni-5Mo steel which  shows  no  pitting in  1N HC1 at  room temperature. Mo(VI) oxide which  is contained in  Cr(IlI) oxyhydroxide in  the state of  solid solution is  suppressed from  its transpassive dissolution and  contributes

· to  the  remarkably increased resistance to  attacks of  CI-  and  acids due  to  the  stability of  Mo(VI) oxide  by its nature in  acid  solution.

(4)  The thickness of  the  passive film  of  C r - N i   steels increases with  increase in  M o content both  in  H~SO4  and  HCI solutions and  further, the  d.c. resistance of  the  passive film  of  a Cr-Ni steel alloyed with  Mo  in  HCI  solution is  larger than  that in  H~SO4 solution, although  the  thickness of  the  film  formed in  HCI  solution is  thinner than that  of  the  film  formed in  H~SO4  solution. These  facts also  show one of  the  reasons that the  addition of  Mo  to  stainless steel is  effective for  the  inhibition of  pitting in  HCI solution.
